🛢️ Petroleum Storage & Transport Rules (India)

Sarat Rout April 4, 2026 8 min read Industrial Safety & Law
Petroleum rules classify inflammable liquids into Class A, B, C with varying safety requirements

Under the Petroleum Act, 1934 and the Petroleum Rules, 2002 (as amended), the storage, transport, and handling of petroleum in India is strictly regulated by the Chief Controller of Explosives through the Petroleum and Explosives Safety Organisation (PESO). Every litre of petroleum — from petrol at a filling station to furnace oil at an industrial plant — falls within one of three hazard classes, each carrying its own container standards, air space requirements, licence exemptions, and criminal penalties for non-compliance.

This guide breaks down those classes and their obligations in plain terms, so that dealers, transporters, inspectors, and legal practitioners can quickly determine what applies and when a licence is mandatory.

1. Regulatory Framework

Governing Legislation

The Petroleum Act, 1934 (Sections 4, 5, 6, 8, 11) and the Petroleum Rules, 2002 (Chapters II, III, V, VI) form the complete statutory framework. The Chief Controller of Explosives administers the Act through PESO, with powers to inspect premises, approve container designs, grant exemptions, and prosecute violations. Non-compliance attracts imprisonment and fines under Section 10 of the Petroleum Act.

Applicability: Whole of India. Defence Forces of India hold special exemptions in all three classes.

Class A & B storage — approved steel containers with mandatory air space
PESO-approved storage sites must comply with class-specific container thickness and spacing rules

2. Class A — Highly Inflammable

🔴 Class A

Examples: Petrol (Motor Spirit), Naphtha, Aviation Turbine Fuel

Flash Point < 23 °C

Container Requirements

Material

Must be tinned, galvanised, or rust-proof steel/iron, approved by the Chief Controller of Explosives. Glass bottles permitted only up to 2.5 litres (laboratory use, specifically approved).

Design & Safety

Airtight screw plugs or caps. Must be leak-proof, durable, and maintained in good condition at all times.

Air Space

Minimum 5% free air space must be maintained inside every filled container to allow for thermal expansion.

Labelling

Must clearly display: "Petrol / Motor Spirit — Highly Inflammable"

Capacity & Minimum Sheet Thickness

Licence Exemption

≤ 30 L

No licence required for quantities up to 30 litres (not for sale). Glass/stoneware containers must not exceed 1 litre each; metal containers must not exceed 25 litres each.

Motor Vehicle Exemption

Fuel already in a vehicle tank requires no licence. Up to 100 litres extra Class A petroleum may be stored for own vehicle use. If storing more than 30 L, it must be kept in an isolated location away from buildings.

Defence Forces Exception

Container rules under Class A do not apply to containers owned and operated by the Defence Forces of India.

3. Class B — Moderately Inflammable

🟡 Class B

Examples: Kerosene, Diesel (certain grades), Aviation Spirit

Flash Point 23 °C – 65 °C

Container Material

Must be approved steel or iron containers only.

Air Space

Minimum 5% free air space required in each container — same standard as Class A.

Dispatch & Delivery Rules

Class B petroleum can only be lawfully delivered to:

Licence Holders

A valid licence holder or their legally authorised agent.

Port / Railway Authorities

Port authorities or railway administrations as per transit rules.

Exempt Persons

Any person legally authorised to possess petroleum without a licence.

Special Delivery Without Licence
  • Class B petroleum can be delivered without a licence if the quantity does not exceed 15,000 litres, provided it is packed in sealed, airtight approved containers.
  • The receiver must ensure immediate disposal or use of the petroleum in the original sealed packages.
  • If the quantity exceeds 2,500 litres, a dry-chemical fire extinguisher must be provided on site.

Decantation Rule (Kerosene)

Permitted for licence holders only. Kerosene must be transferred into containers not exceeding 2,500 litres immediately upon decantation.

Licence Exemption

≤ 2,500 L

No licence required for storage or transport if total quantity ≤ 2,500 litres AND no single container exceeds 1,000 litres.

Exceptions (Class B)

Rules do not apply to Defence Forces of India, or to kerosene specifically supplied for licensed decantation purposes.

4. Class C — Less Volatile Petroleum

🟢 Class C

Examples: Furnace Oil, Lubricating Oil, High-Speed Diesel (certain grades)

Flash Point > 65 °C

Container Material

Must be approved steel or iron containers only.

Air Space

Minimum 3% free air space required — reduced from 5% due to lower volatility and reduced expansion risk.

Licence Exemption

≤ 45,000 L

No licence required if total quantity stored or transported does not exceed 45,000 litres, provided containers comply with approved standards.

Exceptions (Class C)

Container rules do not apply to containers owned and operated by the Defence Forces of India.

5. General Restrictions on Delivery

⚠️ Delivery Obligations — All Classes

Petroleum cannot be delivered to just anyone. The delivery must match the receiver's licence type and the quantity permitted under their licence. The sender is legally responsible for ensuring safe handling and legal compliance at the point of delivery.

Delivering petroleum to an unlicensed person (where a licence is required), or in quantities exceeding the permitted threshold, is a criminal offence under Section 10 of the Petroleum Act attracting both imprisonment and fine.

7. Statutory References

Governing Statutes & Rules

These rules derive from the Petroleum Act, 1934 (Sections 4, 5, 6, 8, 11) and Petroleum Rules, 2002 (Chapters II, III, V, VI). The Chief Controller of Explosives has powers to enforce conditions, inspect premises, approve container designs, and grant exemptions in special circumstances.

Non-compliance attracts penalties including imprisonment and fines under Section 10 of the Petroleum Act.

Always verify the latest amendments through PESO (Petroleum and Explosives Safety Organisation) official notifications at peso.gov.in.
