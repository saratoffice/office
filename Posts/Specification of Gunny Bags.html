TEXTILES — LIGHT WEIGHT JUTE SACKING BAGS FOR PACKING 50 kg FOODGRAINS — SPECIFICATION

Technical Standards Board February 25, 2025 15 min read Technical

This section outlines the Scope, References, Terminology, and Manufacturing Requirements for lightweight jute sacking bags used for packing 50 kg foodgrains. It also addresses the environmental criteria and standards for labeling the product as "environmentally friendly." Below is a breakdown of each section:

1. SCOPE

This standard specifies the constructional details and other requirements for lightweight jute sacking bags that are intended for packing 50 kg foodgrains. These requirements ensure the bags' durability, strength, and suitability for foodgrain packaging.

2. REFERENCES

This section lists the standards referred to in the document that are essential for the manufacturing, packaging, and testing of jute bags. These references include:

  • IS 2873:1991 – Textiles: Packaging of jute products in bales.
  • IS 5476:1986 – Glossary of terms related to jute.
  • IS 9113:1993 – General requirements for jute sacking.

These standards provide the foundation for ensuring the bags meet the necessary criteria for strength, packaging, and labelling.

3. TERMINOLOGY

This section clarifies that the definitions given in IS 5476 (Glossary of terms related to jute) will apply to this standard. This helps establish a clear understanding of the terms used throughout the document, especially those related to jute materials and construction.

4. MANUFACTURE

This section covers the manufacturing requirements for the jute bags.

4.1 Construction

  • Type A: Bags made from single warp, double weft, woven on a modern shuttleless loom.
  • Type B: Bags made from double warp, single weft, woven on a conventional shuttle loom.

The bags should either have a single blue stripe along the length of the bag, or no stripe, as agreed between the buyer and seller. These construction specifications must comply with the details outlined in Table 1.

4.2 Seam

The bags' two sides should be sewn using herakle stitches on the selvedge through two layers of sacking, in accordance with IS 9113. The number of stitches per decimeter should be 10 ± 1.

4.3 Safety Stitch

If agreed between the buyer and seller, a line of safety union stitches should be provided at the inner edges of the herakle stitches. The number of safety union stitches per decimeter should also be 10 ± 1.

4.4 Hemming at the Mouth

The mouth of the bag should be hemmed as specified in IS 9113.

4.5 Freedom from Defects

The bags must meet the requirement of freedom from major defects as outlined in Annex A.

5. SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS

This section outlines additional specific criteria that the bags and packaging must meet:

5.1 Bags Conform to Table 1

The bags must conform to the requirements specified in Table 1, which likely includes characteristics such as dimensions, strength, stitching, and other construction specifications.

5.2 Bales Conform to Table 2

The bales containing the bags must meet the requirements in Table 2, which may include specifications for packaging, weight, and bundle sizes.

5.3 Contract Moisture Regain

The bags must have a contract moisture regain of 20 percent. This value represents the moisture content that the jute bags are allowed to retain during normal conditions.

5.4 Additional Requirements for Labelling as Environmentally Friendly Products

This section sets the criteria for environmentally friendly products and their labeling.

5.4.1 General Requirement

5.4.1.1: The bags must meet the quality and performance requirements as outlined in sections 5.1 to 5.3.

5.4.1.2: The manufacturer must provide environmental consent clearance from the State Pollution Control Board and comply with the Environment (Products) Act, 1986, as well as the Factory Act, 1948. This includes proof of compliance with noise levels and occupational health regulations.

5.4.1.3: The product packaging may include brief details about the environmental criteria based on which the product is labeled as environmentally friendly.

  • Outside Length (cm): The total length of the jute bag from one end to the other. 94 cm for both types with tolerance +4 cm and -0 cm.

Marking Requirements (Clause 7)

  • The bales must be marked in accordance with IS 2873, and additional markings may be required by the buyer or by regulations.
  • BIS Certification Marking: The bales may carry the Standard Mark.
  • The bales may also carry the ECO-Mark if the relevant requirements in Clause 5.4 are satisfied.

Sampling and Criteria for Conformity (Clause 8)

1. Lot Definition:

A lot consists of all bales of jute bags of the same size produced under similar conditions of production and delivered under one dispatch note.

2. Sample Size and Testing for Conformity:

For assessing conformity, bales are selected at random, and the sample will be tested for characteristics like gross mass of bales, tare mass (weight of bailing hoops and packing materials), and number of bundles per bale.

3. Criteria for Conformity:

The lot is considered conforming if the corrected net mass of the sample bales meets the required contract mass, and the number of bags per bale matches the requirement. If all these conditions are met, the bales will be tested further for additional properties like freedom from defects, length, width, moisture regain, breaking strength, and oil content.

Criteria for Conformity for Various Tests:

  • Freedom from Defects: A bag is considered defective if it contains two or more major defects. The lot will conform if the number of defective bags is less than or equal to the acceptance number specified in the table.
  • Length, Width, Ends/dm, Picks/dm, Moisture Regain: A bag is defective if it fails any of these requirements. The lot is conforming if the number of defective bags is within the acceptance number.
  • Mass per Bag: The average mass per bag should meet or exceed the specified value. No more than 10% of the bags can have a mass below the specified lower value.
  • Breaking Strength: The average breaking strengths for warpway, weftway, and seam strength should meet or exceed the specified values.
  • Oil Content: Two bags are tested from two different bales, and the lot is conforming if both bags meet the oil content requirement.
