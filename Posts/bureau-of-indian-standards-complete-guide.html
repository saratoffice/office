BIS - Ensuring Quality, Safety & Reliability of Indian Products

What is the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)?

The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) is the national standards body of India responsible for setting quality standards for goods, services, and systems in the country. It plays a crucial role in ensuring the quality, safety, and efficiency of products consumed by Indian citizens.

Historical Background

1947

Indian Standards Institution (ISI) was established to set up and standardize quality norms for goods and services in India.

2016

Bureau of Indian Standards Act, 2016 was enacted, replacing the previous BIS Act, 1986.

12 Oct 2017

BIS was established under the Bureau of Indian Standards Act, 2016, which came into effect on this date. BIS succeeded the Indian Standards Institution (ISI).

Headquarters: New Delhi, India

üîß Key Activities of BIS

BIS is involved in a broad range of activities to ensure the adherence to quality standards across various industries in India. The major activities include:

1. Standards Formulation

BIS formulates standards for industrial goods, consumer products, and services through experts from industry, government, and academia.

Categories: Product standards, Service standards, Management system standards

2. Product Certification

BIS issues product certification to ensure products meet prescribed quality standards. Certified products can use the ISI MARK - a symbol of quality assurance.

Compulsory Registration Scheme (CRS) for specific products ensures safety and quality before market entry.

3. Management System Certification

BIS provides certification for management systems (ISO 9001, ISO 14001, ISO 45001), verifying organizations meet international standards.

4. Hallmarking

BIS manages the Hallmarking Scheme for precious metals (gold and silver), guaranteeing purity and protecting consumers from fraud.

BIS Hallmarking for Gold & Silver

The BIS Hallmark is a certification mark that guarantees the purity of gold and silver jewelry. The hallmarking process involves:

  • Checking the purity of the metal
  • Verifying the manufacturer's details
  • Issuing certification with purity grade (e.g., 916 for 22K gold)

This is essential for protecting consumers from fraud and ensuring that jewelry sold in the market meets quality standards.

üìã BIS Rules and Regulations (2017)

The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) Rules, 2017 are the regulations under which BIS operates. These rules are designed to provide the operational framework for the BIS Act, 2016, and ensure that BIS fulfills its responsibilities effectively.

Key Provisions of BIS Rules, 2017:

1. Functions of BIS

  • Formulation of standards for various sectors including food products, electronics, textiles, construction, and automobiles
  • Certification of products and management systems
  • Consumer awareness on the importance of product standards and quality
  • Monitoring and regulating BIS certifications, ensuring conformity to standards

2. Process of Certification

  • Manufacturers must apply for product certification or management system certification by submitting their products or systems for inspection and testing
  • If the product complies with the relevant BIS standards, the company is granted certification and allowed to use the ISI mark for product labeling
  • The certification can be suspended or revoked if there are violations or deviations from the standards

3. Compulsory Certification

The Compulsory Registration Scheme (CRS) is enforced by BIS, mandating certain products (e.g., electronics, toys, cables, etc.) to be certified before they are allowed to be sold in India.

This ensures that products that affect public health and safety meet national quality norms.

4. Role of BIS Advisory Committees

BIS is supported by advisory committees consisting of representatives from the industry, government, and academia. These committees play an important role in recommending the formulation and revision of standards and regulations.

5. Penalties for Non-Compliance

The BIS Act and rules provide provisions for penalties against individuals or entities that violate the standards or misrepresent BIS-certified products.

For example, manufacturers who misuse the ISI mark without certification are subject to penalties under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019.

6. International Cooperation

BIS collaborates with international standardization bodies such as the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC).

This helps align Indian standards with international best practices, enhancing global trade and ensuring consumer safety.

Conclusion

The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) is a vital institution for maintaining high-quality standards for products and services in India. The BIS Act, 2016, and BIS Rules, 2017 enable it to perform its functions effectively, ensuring consumer protection, supporting industry standards, and promoting international competitiveness.

Through its wide array of activities such as product certification, standards formulation, and hallmarking, BIS plays a significant role in safeguarding public welfare and ensuring the credibility of products in the marketplace.
