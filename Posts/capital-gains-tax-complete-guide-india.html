Capital Gains Tax: Complete Guide to Taxation on Assets, Mutual Funds & Fixed Deposits

Genus Power February 17, 2025 10 min read Finance & Taxation
Understanding capital gains tax for smarter investment decisions

This article provides a comprehensive overview of capital gains and the associated taxes, covering different types of assets, holding periods, and tax treatment. Here's a concise breakdown of the main concepts to help you navigate the complexities of capital gains taxation in India.

1. What is a Capital Gain?

A capital gain is the profit from the sale of a capital asset, such as property, mutual funds, or stocks.

This profit is considered income and is taxed in the year of the sale. Capital gains tax can either be short-term or long-term, depending on the holding period of the asset.

2. Capital Assets

A capital asset is any property held by an individual, like:

Land, building, house property
Vehicles
Machinery
Patents, trademarks
Shares, mutual fund units
Jewelry

Not Capital Assets

  • Stocks (inventory)
  • Raw materials
  • Personal goods like clothes
  • Furniture

3. Short-Term vs. Long-Term Capital Assets

Short-term Capital Assets

  • Held for less than 36 months
  • For immovable property (land, building): less than 24 months (since FY 2017-18)
  • Equity shares & mutual funds: less than 12 months

Long-term Capital Assets

  • Held for more than 36 months
  • Immovable property: more than 24 months
  • Equity shares & mutual funds: more than 12 months

4. Capital Gains Tax Rates

Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG)

With STT (Securities Transaction Tax):

15%

Without STT:

Taxed at applicable income tax slab rate

Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG)

Equity-oriented assets:

10% if gains exceed Rs. 1 lakh (no indexation)

Other assets:

20% with indexation benefit

5. Indexation Benefit

What is Indexation?

When calculating long-term capital gains, you can adjust the cost of acquisition and cost of improvement using indexation, which accounts for inflation. This reduces the taxable capital gain significantly.

Indexed Cost of Acquisition = Cost of Acquisition × (CII of year of sale / CII of year of purchase)

CII = Cost Inflation Index (notified by CBDT each year)

6. Taxation on Mutual Funds

Debt Funds

Short-term Taxed at slab rate

Long-term 20% with indexation

Equity Funds

Short-term 15%

Long-term No tax on gains up to Rs. 1 lakh (post-2018), 10% above that

7. Tax on Fixed Deposits (FDs)

Fixed Deposit Interest Taxation

Interest earned on fixed deposits (FDs) is added to your total income and taxed according to your tax slab.

TDS on FD Interest

If interest exceeds Rs. 10,000 in a year:

  • TDS deducted by bank at 10% (20% if PAN not provided)

8. Calculating Capital Gains

Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG)

STCG = Full Value Consideration − Expenditure − Cost of Acquisition − Cost of Improvement

Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG)

LTCG = Full Value Consideration − Expenditure − Indexed Cost of Acquisition − Indexed Cost of Improvement − Exemptions

9. Avoiding TDS on Fixed Deposits

Form 15G / 15H

If your total income is below the taxable threshold, you can avoid TDS on fixed deposits by submitting:

Form 15G

For non-senior citizens

Form 15H

For senior citizens

Submit these forms at the start of the financial year to ensure the bank doesn't deduct TDS on your interest income.

Quick Reference: Capital Gains Tax Rates

Asset Type Holding Period Tax Rate
Equity Shares (STT paid) < 12 months 15%
Equity Shares (STT paid) > 12 months 10% (over ₹1L)
Immovable Property < 24 months Slab rate
Immovable Property > 24 months 20% with indexation

Debt Mutual Funds > 36 months 20% with indexation

Key Takeaways

  • Holding period determines whether gains are short-term or long-term
  • Indexation reduces tax burden on long-term gains by accounting for inflation
  • Equity-oriented assets enjoy lower tax rates (15% STCG, 10% LTCG over ₹1L)
  • Debt funds and other assets taxed at 20% with indexation for long-term
  • Submit Form 15G/15H to avoid TDS on FD interest if income below taxable limit

Conclusion

This breakdown highlights the essential aspects of capital gains taxation and provides clarity on how different assets and holding periods influence tax liabilities. Understanding these concepts helps in:

  • Planning investment exits strategically
  • Optimizing tax outgo through indexation
  • Managing TDS on fixed deposit interest
  • Making informed decisions between debt and equity funds

Always consult with a tax professional for advice specific to your financial situation.
