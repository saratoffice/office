Capital Gains Tax: Complete Guide to Taxation on Assets, Mutual Funds & Fixed Deposits

Updated for FY 2025-26 — including Finance Act 2024 changes effective from 23 July 2024: new STCG/LTCG rates, ₹1.25 lakh exemption, and indexation overhaul

Sarat Rout February 17, 2025 14 min read Finance & Taxation
Understanding capital gains tax — updated for the Finance Act 2024 changes effective from 23 July 2024

The Finance (No. 2) Act, 2024, announced on 23 July 2024, fundamentally restructured India's capital gains tax framework. If you invest in equity shares, mutual funds, property, gold, or hold fixed deposits, the rules you relied on before July 2024 have changed significantly — new tax rates, a revised exemption threshold, and the removal of indexation for most assets. This guide provides a complete, updated reference for FY 2025-26.

Key Alert — 23 July 2024 Pivot Date: The Finance Act 2024 changed STCG rate on equity from 15% → 20%, LTCG rate from 10% → 12.5%, LTCG exemption from ₹1 lakh → ₹1.25 lakh, and removed indexation for most asset transfers on or after this date. Transactions before 23 July 2024 are taxed under the old regime.

1. What is a Capital Gain?

Capital Gain — Definition

A capital gain is the profit arising from the transfer (sale, exchange, or relinquishment) of a capital asset. The gain is the difference between the sale consideration received and the cost of acquisition and improvement. Capital gains are treated as income in the year of transfer and taxed under the Income Tax Act, 1961 — now significantly restructured by the Finance (No. 2) Act, 2024.

Capital gains are classified into Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) and Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) based on the holding period of the asset — and different tax rates apply to each category.

12.5% LTCG — All assets (S.112/112A)

2. Capital Assets — What Qualifies and What Doesn't

A capital asset is broadly any property held by a person — whether moveable or immoveable, tangible or intangible. The following are capital assets for tax purposes:

Land, building, house property
Listed equity shares, MF units
Jewellery, precious metals & stones
Patents, trademarks, copyrights
Machinery and plant
Gold, silver, bullion
Unlisted shares and debentures
Agricultural land (non-rural)

Not Capital Assets — No Capital Gains Tax

  • Stock-in-trade (inventory held for business sale)
  • Raw materials and consumables used in business/profession
  • Personal goods — clothing, furniture for personal use
  • Agricultural land in rural areas (as defined under Section 2(14))
  • 6½% Gold Bonds, 7% Gold Bonds, National Defence Gold Bonds
  • Special Bearer Bonds, Gold Deposit Bonds under the Gold Deposit Scheme

3. Holding Periods — STCG vs LTCG Classification

Short-Term Capital Asset

  • Listed equity shares, equity MF units, business trust units: held for ≤ 12 months
  • Immovable property (land, building): held for ≤ 24 months
  • All other assets (gold, unlisted shares, debt MFs*): held for ≤ 36 months

* Debt MFs acquired on or after 01 April 2023 are always short-term regardless of holding period (Section 50AA).

Long-Term Capital Asset

  • Listed equity shares, equity MF units, business trust units: held for > 12 months
  • Immovable property (land, building): held for > 24 months
  • All other assets (gold, unlisted shares): held for > 36 months

Long-term status entitles the asset to lower tax rates and (in limited cases) indexation benefits.

5. Indexation — What Changed in 2024

Indexation — Before and After 23 July 2024

Indexation adjusts the cost of acquisition for inflation using the Cost Inflation Index (CII) notified by CBDT each year, reducing the taxable capital gain. It was previously available for all long-term assets taxed under Section 112 at 20%.

Indexed Cost of Acquisition = Cost of Acquisition × (CII of year of sale ÷ CII of year of purchase)
CII = Cost Inflation Index, notified annually by CBDT under the Income Tax Act
Asset / ScenarioBefore 23 Jul 2024After 23 Jul 2024
Listed equity, equity MF (LTCG — S.112A) 10% — no indexation 12.5% — no indexation
Property, gold, unlisted shares (LTCG — S.112) 20% with indexation OR 10% without 12.5% — no indexation (uniform)
Property acquired before 23 Jul 2024 — resident individual/HUF 20% with indexation Choose: 12.5% (no indexation) OR 20% (with indexation) — pick lower tax
Property acquired on or after 23 Jul 2024 Not applicable 12.5% only — no indexation available

6. Calculating Capital Gains — Formulas

Short-Term Capital Gain (STCG)

STCG = Full Value of Consideration
− Transfer Expenditure
− Cost of Acquisition
− Cost of Improvement

Indexation does not apply to STCG. Gains are either taxed at 20% flat (equity) or added to income (other assets).

Long-Term Capital Gain (LTCG)

LTCG = Full Value of Consideration
− Transfer Expenditure
− Indexed/Actual Cost of Acquisition
− Indexed/Actual Cost of Improvement
− Eligible Exemptions (Ss. 54, 54F, 54EC)

Indexation applies only to pre-23 Jul 2024 immovable property for resident individuals/HUFs (optional).

8. Tax on Fixed Deposits and TDS Rules (FY 2025-26)

Fixed Deposit Interest — Taxability

Interest earned on fixed deposits (FDs) is not a capital gain — it is taxed as Income from Other Sources at your applicable income tax slab rate. All FD interest must be reported in your ITR in the year it accrues (not just at maturity).

Banks deduct Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) under Section 194A of the Income Tax Act if the interest in a financial year exceeds the threshold. The TDS amount is adjusted against your total tax liability when you file your ITR.

9. Avoiding TDS — Form 15G / 15H

If your total taxable income is below the basic exemption limit, you can submit a self-declaration form to your bank at the start of each financial year to prevent TDS deduction on FD interest. Submit separately to each bank where you hold FDs.

Form 15G / Form 15H — Self-Declaration for TDS Exemption

Form 15G

For individuals below 60 years and HUFs. Taxable income (including FD interest) must be below the basic exemption limit — ₹2.5 lakh (old regime) or ₹4 lakh (new regime). Interest income must not exceed the basic exemption limit itself.

Form 15H

For senior citizens aged 60 years and above. Total income must be below the taxable threshold (₹3 lakh for 60–80 age group; more for super seniors). No condition on interest income exceeding the limit — more flexible than 15G.

Important (from April 2026): A new unified Form 121 will replace both Form 15G and Form 15H effective 1 April 2026, per the Income Tax Department's announcement.

10. Capital Gains Exemptions — Sections 54, 54F, 54EC

These reinvestment exemptions (rollover benefits) remain unchanged under the Finance Act 2024 — taxpayers can still reduce or defer LTCG tax by reinvesting in specified assets:

Section 54
Reinvest in House Property

LTCG from sale of a residential house is exempt if reinvested in one (or up to two, if gain ≤ ₹2 crore) new residential house property within specified timelines. Cap: up to ₹10 crore of gain.

Section 54F
Long-Term Assets → House Property

LTCG from sale of any long-term capital asset other than a residential house is exempt if the entire net sale consideration is reinvested in a residential house property. Proportionate exemption if partial reinvestment.

Section 54EC
Specified Bonds (NHAI, REC)

LTCG from transfer of land or building is exempt if invested in specified bonds (NHAI, REC) within 6 months of transfer. Cap: ₹50 lakh per financial year. Lock-in: 5 years.

Section 54B
Agricultural Land

LTCG from transfer of agricultural land exempt if reinvested in another agricultural land within 2 years. Available to individuals and HUFs using land for agriculture for 2 years prior to transfer.

Key Takeaways — FY 2025-26

  • STCG on listed equity and equity MF is now 20% (up from 15%) — effective 23 Jul 2024 (Section 111A)
  • LTCG on all assets is now a uniform 12.5% without indexation (up from 10%) — effective 23 Jul 2024
  • LTCG exemption increased from ₹1 lakh to ₹1.25 lakh per FY — applies only to equity (Section 112A)
  • Indexation removed for most assets — exception only for pre-23 Jul 2024 immovable property in resident individual/HUF hands
  • Debt MFs purchased on or after 01 Apr 2023 are always STCG at slab rate — no LTCG benefit possible
  • TDS threshold on FD interest raised from ₹40,000 → ₹50,000 (FY 2025-26) for regular citizens; ₹50,000 → ₹1 lakh for senior citizens
  • Exemption rollover benefits under Sections 54, 54F, and 54EC remain unchanged
  • Form 121 to replace Form 15G/15H from 1 April 2026
