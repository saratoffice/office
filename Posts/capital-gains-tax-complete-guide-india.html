Capital Gains Tax: Complete Guide to Taxation on Assets, Mutual Funds & Fixed Deposits

Sarat Rout February 17, 2025 10 min read Finance & Taxation
Understanding capital gains tax for smarter investment decisions

This article provides a comprehensive overview of capital gains and the associated taxes, covering different types of assets, holding periods, and tax treatment. Here's a concise breakdown of the main concepts to help you navigate the complexities of capital gains taxation in India.

1. What is a Capital Gain?

A capital gain is the profit from the sale of a capital asset, such as property, mutual funds, or stocks.

This profit is considered income and is taxed in the year of the sale. Capital gains tax can either be short-term or long-term, depending on the holding period of the asset.

2. Capital Assets

A capital asset is any property held by an individual, like:

Land, building
Vehicles
Machinery
Patents
Shares
Jewelry

Not Capital Assets

  • Stock-in-trade (inventory)
  • Raw materials
  • Personal effects (clothes, furniture)
  • Agricultural land in rural areas
  • 6.5% Gold Bonds, 1977
  • Special Bearer Bonds, 1991

3. Short-Term vs. Long-Term Capital Assets

Short-term Capital Assets

  • Held for less than 36 months (most assets)
  • Immovable property: less than 24 months
  • Equity shares & equity funds: less than 12 months

Long-term Capital Assets

  • Held for more than 36 months (most assets)
  • Immovable property: more than 24 months
  • Equity shares & equity funds: more than 12 months

5. Indexation Benefit

What is Indexation?

Indexation adjusts the purchase cost of an asset for inflation using the Cost Inflation Index (CII). This reduces taxable gains significantly for long-term assets.

Indexed Cost of Acquisition = Original Cost × (CII of sale year / CII of purchase year)

Important: CII is notified by CBDT each year. Base year is 2001-02 (CII=100).

6. Taxation on Mutual Funds

Debt Funds

  • STCG Taxed at slab rate
  • LTCG 20% with indexation
  • Holding period: >36 months for LTCG

Equity Funds

  • STCG 15%
  • LTCG 10% over ₹1L/year
  • Holding period: >12 months for LTCG

7. Tax on Fixed Deposits (FDs)

Fixed Deposit Interest Taxation

Interest earned on fixed deposits is added to your total income and taxed according to your income tax slab.

TDS on FD Interest

  • If interest exceeds ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens) in a year
  • TDS deducted by bank at 10% (20% if PAN not provided)
  • CORRECTION: Threshold is ₹40,000 (not ₹10,000 as per Budget 2019)

8. Calculating Capital Gains

Short-Term Capital Gains

STCG = Sale Price − (Purchase Cost + Improvement Cost + Expenses)

Long-Term Capital Gains

LTCG = Sale Price − (Indexed Cost + Indexed Improvement + Expenses) − Exemptions u/s 54, 54EC, etc.

Important Corrections & Updates

FD TDS Threshold

Corrected: TDS on FD interest applies if interest exceeds ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens), not ₹10,000 (old rule).

Surcharge & Cess

Tax rates exclude surcharge (if income > ₹50L) and 4% health & education cess. Always calculate net tax accordingly.

Listed vs Unlisted Shares

Unlisted shares: LTCG >24 months, taxed at 20% with indexation. STCG taxed at slab rate.

Grandfathering Clause

Equity LTCG up to 31 Jan 2018 is grandfathered - cost deemed as higher of actual cost or FMV as on 31 Jan 2018.

9. Avoiding TDS on Fixed Deposits

Form 15G

For individuals below 60 years whose total income is below taxable limit.

Form 15H

For senior citizens (60+) with income below taxable limit.

Submit these forms at the start of each financial year to prevent TDS deduction. Can be submitted online through net banking or physically at the bank branch.

Key Exemptions for LTCG

Section 54

Exemption on sale of residential house if proceeds invested in another house

Section 54EC

Invest in specified bonds (like NHAI/REC) up to ₹50L within 6 months

Section 54F

Sale of any asset other than house, invest in house for exemption

Key Takeaways

  • Holding period is crucial - determines STCG vs LTCG
  • Indexation reduces tax burden significantly for long-term assets
  • Equity-oriented assets have concessional tax rates
  • FD TDS threshold is now ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens)
  • Submit Form 15G/15H to avoid TDS if income below taxable limit
  • Unlisted shares have different tax treatment than listed

Conclusion

Understanding capital gains taxation helps in:

  • Strategic exit planning to minimize tax
  • Availing indexation benefits for long-term holdings
  • Choosing between debt and equity funds wisely
  • Planning reinvestments to claim exemptions

This guide reflects tax rules applicable for FY 2024-25. Tax laws change frequently - consult a qualified CA for personalized advice.
