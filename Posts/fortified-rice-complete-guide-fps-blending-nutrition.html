1. What Is Food Fortification?

Definition

Food fortification is the practice of deliberately increasing the content of essential micronutrients — vitamins and minerals — in a food to improve its nutritional quality and provide a public health benefit with minimal risk to health.

It is mandated and regulated by the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI), which notifies standards on the type, level, and form of nutrients to be added.

India currently fortifies five major food commodities distributed through government schemes:

Milk 🥛 Oil 🛢️ Wheat 🌾 Rice 🍚 Salt 🧂
Fortified Rice (+F)

Fortified with Iron, Folic Acid, and Vitamin B12. The official slogan: "Sampoorna Poshan, Swasth Jeevan" — Complete Nutrition, Healthy Life.

Fortified Rice Kernels (FRK) — extruded rice-shaped kernels blended 1:100 with normal rice
FPS dealers play a critical role — distributing, explaining, and educating beneficiaries on fortified rice

3. What Is Fortified Rice & How Is It Made?

Fortified rice is normal rice blended with Fortified Rice Kernels (FRK). FRKs are rice-shaped kernels produced through an extrusion process — rice flour is mixed with a vitamin and mineral premix, forced through a die to create rice-shaped granules, and then dried. These kernels are visually similar to regular rice grains and are blended in at the mill.

Manufacturing Process

Rice flour + Premix (Iron, Folic Acid, Vitamin B12) → Extrusion through die → FRK granules (rice-shaped) → Dried → Blended with normal rice at mill using dynamic blending machines at 1:100 ratio

Key Characteristics

Fortified rice has the same taste, same texture, same cooking properties, and the same price as normal rice. It strengthens nutritional security through PDS, ICDS, and MDM without requiring any change in consumer behaviour.

4. Nutritional Content in FRK

As per Government of India guidelines, each kilogram of Fortified Rice Kernels must contain the following nutrients:

Iron
28 – 42.5 mg/kg

Prevents anaemia; supports haemoglobin production and oxygen transport in blood

Folic Acid
75 – 125 µg/kg

Supports blood formation, maternal health, foetal development, and prevention of neural tube defects

Vitamin B12
0.75 – 1.25 µg/kg

Supports nerve health, red blood cell production, and DNA synthesis

5. Health, Quality & Taste Benefits

Health Benefits

  • Iron — Prevents anaemia; improves energy and immunity
  • Folic Acid — Supports pregnancy health, blood formation, child growth
  • Vitamin B12 — Helps in blood production and nervous system functioning
  • Daily consumption improves strength, protects health, prevents hidden hunger

No Change in Experience

  • Same taste as normal rice
  • Same price — no extra cost to beneficiaries
  • Same cooking method — no special preparation needed
  • Same shelf life — 12 months under proper storage
  • Safe for all age groups including infants and pregnant women

6. Blending Ratio

1 : 100
1 part FRK blended with 100 parts normal rice (1%)

Example: 1 kg FRK is blended with 100 kg of normal rice at the rice mill using special dynamic blending machines to ensure uniform mixing throughout the batch.

7. Blending Efficiency Test (Kernel Count Test)

To verify correct blending, a kernel count test is conducted at the mill at regular intervals:

1

Conduct tests every 2 hours during blending operations

2

Collect samples from 4–6 bags from the current batch

3

Weigh 100 g of fortified rice from the combined sample

4

Separate FRKs carefully under good light — they are slightly different in colour/texture

5

Count the FRKs and record in the blending register

8. Storage & Quality Maintenance at FPS

Storage — General Guidelines

Store like normal rice — in a cool, dry place. Keep away from pesticides, chemicals, kerosene, and fertilizers. Protect from insects and rodents. Do not expose to direct sunlight.

Godown Requirements

  • Walls and ceilings — free from seepage
  • Walls — well-painted and without cracks
  • Adequate ventilation and natural light
  • Clean and pest-free premises

Stacking Rules

  • Keep stacks away from walls and roof
  • Maintain space between stacks for cleaning and air circulation
  • Store rice above the floor on wooden pallets or tarpaulin
  • Do not leave bags open — remove air, tie with string, store properly
Shelf Life

12 months — same as normal rice under proper storage conditions. There is no change in taste, colour, smell, or cooking method during this period.

9. Role & Responsibility of FPS Dealers

FPS Dealer Duties

FPS dealers are not just distributors — they are the primary point of beneficiary education on fortified rice. Their active engagement determines whether the nutritional benefit reaches the household.

Distribution Duties

  • Ensure correct distribution of fortified rice under PDS
  • Show samples to beneficiaries — visual awareness is key
  • Explain the +F logo on packaging and what it means
  • Continue regular duties: ration distribution and register maintenance

Education Duties

  • Explain the health benefits of iron, folic acid, and vitamin B12
  • Clarify: same taste, same price, same cooking method — no concern
  • Educate on proper cooking — do not discard starch water
  • Advise on home storage to preserve quality

10. Storage Instructions for Beneficiaries

1

Keep rice in a clean, dry place away from moisture and heat

2

Avoid tin/zinc containers — they can react with the fortified nutrients

3

Prevent wall moisture — keep walls painted and crack-free

4

Store rice above the floor on wooden planks or tarpaulin — never directly on the ground

5

Do not leave bags open — tie properly after each use to prevent moisture entry and pest access

11. Cooking Procedure

Cook the Same Way as Normal Rice

Fortified rice (with extruded FRK) retains its nutrients after cooking. Washing the rice once or twice does not significantly reduce nutrient content. Do not throw away any kernels that float — these are FRKs and contain the nutrients.

Recommended Method

  • Use a pressure cooker or rice cooker for best results
  • Use measured water — cook with the lid closed
  • No need to boil and drain starch water separately
  • Cooking time is the same as for normal rice

Critical Advice

  • Do NOT throw away starch water — it contains dissolved vitamins and minerals
  • Do not discard floating FRK kernels during washing
  • Do not over-wash — one or two rinses maximum
  • Serve fresh — avoid prolonged reheating

12. Testing & Quality Assurance in Odisha

Odisha follows a multi-stage quality assurance system under the State Food & Civil Supplies Department in coordination with NABL and FSSAI-certified laboratories:

Stage 1: Premix Testing

Nutrient concentration levels checked at the premix manufacturing stage before FRK production begins

Stage 2: FRK Testing

Iron, Folic Acid, and Vitamin B12 content verified in NABL-certified laboratories; shape and physical parameters assessed

Stage 3: Fortified Rice Testing

Random samples tested for blending ratio (1:100), nutrient retention, and full FSSAI compliance at distribution points

🎯 Conclusion

Fortified rice is one of the most powerful tools in India's fight against anaemia and hidden hunger. By enriching the same grain that millions of households already consume — through the same PDS, ICDS, and MDM channels they already trust — the government delivers Iron, Folic Acid, and Vitamin B12 to vulnerable populations at no extra cost and with no change in behaviour.

The role of FPS dealers is far more than distribution. They are frontline educators whose explanation of the +F symbol, the benefits, the cooking method, and the storage guidelines determines whether the nutritional promise of fortified rice actually reaches the kitchen and the plate. With robust multi-stage quality testing and adherence to FSSAI standards, fortified rice delivers on its promise: "Sampoorna Poshan, Swasth Jeevan."
