Google Drive for Desktop: Complete Settings & Configuration Guide

Genus Power February 17, 2025 12 min read Cloud Storage
Optimize your Google Drive for maximum productivity

Introduction

Google Drive for Desktop offers various settings that allow you to configure how files sync, how Google Drive behaves on your computer, and how your photos and videos are uploaded to Google Photos. Below is a complete breakdown of each section and how you can use them effectively for your convenience.

Essential Google Drive Settings

1. Real-Time Presence in Microsoft Office

Collaborate with live visibility

Collaboration Feature

What It Is: This feature allows you to see if someone else is currently editing a shared Microsoft Office file (Word, Excel, PowerPoint) in real-time.

How to Use It:

If you're editing or viewing a shared Office file in Google Drive and someone else is working on it, you'll see a real-time indicator (like a colored dot or their name) showing that they're also editing the document. This is especially useful in team settings, as you can see live updates from multiple users working on the same file.

2. Cache Settings & Local Cached Files Directory

Manage temporary storage location and size

Storage Management

What It Is: Cache settings control how much space on your computer Google Drive can use for temporary files. Cached files allow for faster access to files you've opened recently. The local cached files directory is where Google Drive stores these temporary files on your computer.

Key Options

  • Limit Cache Size: Control maximum storage for cached files
  • Offline Files: Not affected by cache limits

Default Location

C:\Users\<Username>\AppData\Local\Google\DriveFS

Moving Cache to Another Drive

Example: If your C: drive is almost full but you have space on D: drive, change the cache folder location to D:\GoogleDriveCache

Important Notes:

  • No data loss - only temporary files move
  • Performance may be affected on slower drives
  • Ensure plenty of free space on new location

3. Google Drive Streaming Location

Choose how files appear on your computer

Streaming Setup

What It Is: This setting controls where Google Drive files are stored locally on your computer or streamed from the cloud.

Drive Letter

Map Google Drive to a virtual drive letter (like G: or H:), making it behave like any other drive on your computer.

Example: G:\ (15GB visible folder)

Folder

Select a specific folder on your computer to stream Google Drive files to, making it easier to organize.

Example: D:\YourFolderName

4. Show Google Drive in Quick Access (File Explorer)

What It Is: This feature allows Google Drive to show up directly in Quick Access in File Explorer, giving you easy access to your files.

Benefit: Google Drive is treated like a local folder - drag-and-drop files in and out just like any other folder.

5. Google Photos Settings

Control photo and video uploads

Upload Size

Storage Saver Reduced quality to save space

Original Quality Full resolution, no compression

File Types

Upload Screenshots

Upload RAW Files (for photographers)

Pro Tip: Enable RAW upload if you're a photographer working with high-quality image files.

6. Launch on Login

Google Drive automatically starts when you log into your computer.

Files always up-to-date

8. Proxy Settings

Configure connection through proxy servers.

Options: Auto-detect or Direct Connection

Most users leave on Auto-detect

9. Bandwidth Settings

Control upload/download speeds.

Example: Set 2 MB/s limit to prevent interruption of other activities.

10. Configure Hotkeys

Search: Ctrl + Alt + G

Customize combinations to suit your preferences.

11. Help Improve Drive

Send diagnostic data to Google.

Enable if comfortable sharing usage data, disable for privacy.

12. Notification Preferences

Prompt me to back up devices: Get reminders to backup phones, cameras, etc.

Understanding My Drive vs. Other Computers Folders

In Google Drive for Desktop, you may notice two main folders in the Google Drive folder on your computer. These folders represent different aspects of your Google Drive and how they are synced or accessed.

Feature My Drive Other Computers
Location Primary folder for all personal files on Google Drive Represents files from other computers with Drive installed
Sync Status Files sync with Google Drive account and cloud Files synced from other devices you own
Offline Access Can set files to be available offline Files accessible if synced and available locally
File Access Always available unless removed from sync Access files from other devices linked to your account
Use Case Main place to store personal files and sync across devices Access and sync files from other computers or devices

How to Use Both Folders Together

Syncing Files from Other Computers

Ensure Google Drive for Desktop is installed on other computers. Files saved there will appear in Other Computers folder.

Move Files Between Folders

Simply drag and drop files from Other Computers to My Drive to consolidate files from various devices.

Organize Across Devices

Create folders in My Drive and move files from Other Computers into them for a more organized setup.

In Conclusion

My Drive

The primary storage space for your files on Google Drive, where everything you upload or create gets stored and synced. This is the folder you work with most often.

Other Computers

A folder that contains files synced from other devices connected to your Google Drive account. It acts as a bridge to access files stored on those devices.
