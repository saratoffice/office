How to Calculate Income Tax for Salaried Employees

Complete Guide for FY 2025-26 (New Tax Regime) with Updated Slabs & Rebate

Understanding income tax calculation for salaried employees under the new tax regime

How to Calculate Gross Income from Different Sources

To calculate gross income from different income sources with an online income tax calculator, you must know the 5 different sources (or heads) of income. Each of these sources is taxed differently under the Income Tax Act, 1961 [citation:1][citation:5].

📋 The Five Heads of Income Under Income Tax Act, 1961

Income From Business or Profession

Income from all business activities or income generated through a profession must be considered to arrive at the gross income [citation:5].

Salaried Income

To compute the gross income of salaried individuals, you must consider elements such as basic salary, dearness allowance, House Rent Allowance (HRA), gratuity, travel and other allowances [citation:9].

Income From House Property

Rental income from residential and commercial properties falls under this category. The threshold limit for TDS deduction has been enhanced from Rs. 2.40 lakhs for FY 24-25 to Rs. 6.00 lakhs for FY 25-26.

Capital Gains

Profits earned through gains from the sale of assets, such as property, stocks, mutual funds, gold, etc., fall under this category. The gains are categorised as long-term and short-term capital gains [citation:1][citation:5].

Income from Other Sources

Dividends, interest, royalties, gifts, etc., fall under this category. This is the residual head for incomes not taxable under other heads [citation:5].

📢 Key Updates for FY 2025-26 (New Tax Regime)

✅ Standard Deduction Enhanced

In the new tax regime, the standard deduction has been enhanced from Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 75,000 under the Union Budget 2025 [citation:2][citation:4][citation:8]. This is available to all salaried employees and pensioners.

✅ TDS Threshold Changes

  • Rental Income TDS: Threshold increased from Rs. 2.40 lakhs (FY 24-25) to Rs. 6.00 lakhs (FY 25-26)
  • Interest Income TDS (Senior Citizens): Increased from Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 1.00 lakh
  • Interest Income TDS (Others): Increased from Rs. 40,000 to Rs. 50,000
  • Dividend Income TDS: Enhanced from Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 10,000

✅ Capital Gains Tax Changes (Budget 2024 – Applicable for FY 25-26)

  • Long-term capital gains: Uniform rate of 12.5% (previously 20%)
  • Short-term capital gains: 20% on specified assets
  • Indexation benefit: Removed for all assets
  • Exemption threshold for LTCG (equity shares & mutual funds): Increased from Rs. 1.00 lakh to Rs. 1.25 lakh
  • Holding period rationalized: Changed from 12, 24 and 36 months to 12 and 24 months

💰 New Tax Regime Slabs for FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27)

The Union Budget 2025 introduced revised tax slabs under the new tax regime, with the nil tax limit increased to Rs. 4 lakh and a rebate under Section 87A providing zero tax liability for income up to Rs. 12 lakh [citation:2][citation:4][citation:6].

📌 Section 87A Rebate – Zero Tax up to Rs. 12 Lakh

The rebate under Section 87A has been increased to Rs. 60,000 for taxpayers under the new tax regime. This means:

  • If your taxable income is up to Rs. 12,00,000: Your tax liability becomes NIL after claiming the rebate [citation:2][citation:4][citation:6].
  • For salaried employees: With standard deduction of Rs. 75,000, income up to Rs. 12,75,000 becomes tax-free [citation:2][citation:4][citation:8].

Tax Calculation Example (Income Rs. 12,00,000):
Tax on first Rs. 4,00,000 = NIL
Tax on Rs. 4-8 lakh (5% of 4,00,000) = Rs. 20,000
Tax on Rs. 8-12 lakh (10% of 4,00,000) = Rs. 40,000
Total Tax = Rs. 60,000
Less: Section 87A Rebate = (Rs. 60,000)
Net Tax Payable = NIL

🛡️ Marginal Relief for Income Slightly Above Rs. 12 Lakh

For taxpayers with income slightly above Rs. 12 lakh, marginal relief ensures they don't pay excessive tax compared to those earning Rs. 12 lakh or below [citation:6].

Example (Income Rs. 12,10,000):

  • Normal tax payable: Rs. 61,500 (Rs. 20,000 + Rs. 40,000 + 15% of Rs. 10,000)
  • Without marginal relief, this would be the tax.
  • With marginal relief: Tax payable is limited to the excess over Rs. 12 lakh = Rs. 10,000 only

Marginal relief is available for incomes between Rs. 12,00,001 and Rs. 12,75,000 approximately.

📝 How to Calculate Income Tax for Salaried Employees – Step by Step

1

Calculate Your Gross Income from All Sources

In the space provided, enter your total income along with your allowances. The important components of your salary are:

  • Basic Salary
  • Dearness Allowance (DA)
  • House Rent Allowance (HRA)
  • Leave Travel Allowance (LTA)
  • Special Allowances
  • Perquisites

Also include income from other sources like capital gains, interest, rental income, etc. [citation:8].

2

Calculate Exempt Allowances (HRA, LTA, etc.)

Certain allowances are tax-exempt and must be excluded from your gross income.

HRA Exemption Calculation: Take the lowest value among:

  1. Actual HRA received
  2. Actual rent paid minus 10% of (Basic Salary + DA)
  3. 40% of (Basic Salary + DA) for non-metro cities, OR 50% for metro cities

Note: In the new tax regime, HRA exemption is NOT available. The above applies only if you opt for the old tax regime.

LTA Exemption: Available on actual travel bills submitted (not available in new tax regime).

3

Apply Standard Deduction

Under the new tax regime, a standard deduction of Rs. 75,000 is available to all salaried employees and pensioners [citation:2][citation:4][citation:8].

Gross Salary – Standard Deduction = Income from Salary

4

Add Income from Other Heads

Add income from:

  • House Property: Rental income (after 30% standard deduction under Section 24)
  • Capital Gains: Short-term (20%) and Long-term (12.5%) gains as applicable
  • Other Sources: Interest, dividends, etc.
5

Arrive at Gross Total Income

Sum up income from all heads to get your Gross Total Income.

6

Apply Applicable Tax Slabs

Based on your taxable income, apply the new tax regime slabs mentioned above [citation:8][citation:10].

7

Claim Section 87A Rebate (if eligible)

If your total income is up to Rs. 12,00,000, claim the Rs. 60,000 rebate to bring your tax liability to zero [citation:4][citation:6].

8

Add Cess @ 4%

Finally, add Health and Education Cess at 4% on the total tax amount [citation:8].

⏰ Advance Tax – Do You Need to Pay?

Advance tax has to be paid if your estimated income tax liability for the financial year is more than Rs. 10,000 [citation:7].

For salaried employees: If your only income is from salary and your employer has correctly deducted TDS throughout the year, you don't have to worry about paying advance tax [citation:7].

Advance Tax Instalments for FY 2025-26:

  • 15 June: 15% of total tax liability
  • 15 September: 45% (cumulative)
  • 15 December: 75% (cumulative)
  • 15 March: 100% (cumulative)

Note: Certain incomes like capital gains can be paid in the next instalment without penal interest under Section 234C [citation:7].

📝 Summary – Key Takeaways for FY 2025-26

  • Zero tax up to Rs. 12.75 lakh for salaried employees (including standard deduction) under the new tax regime
  • New tax slabs: 0-4L (Nil), 4-8L (5%), 8-12L (10%), 12-16L (15%), 16-20L (20%), 20-24L (25%), >24L (30%)
  • Section 87A rebate of Rs. 60,000 ensures zero tax for income up to Rs. 12 lakh
  • Marginal relief protects those with income just above Rs. 12 lakh
  • Standard deduction increased to Rs. 75,000
  • Capital gains: LTCG @ 12.5% (exemption threshold Rs. 1.25 lakh), STCG @ 20%
  • TDS thresholds increased for rent, interest, and dividend income

Use an online income tax calculator to compare old vs new regime and choose the one that benefits you most. The new tax regime is the default regime, but you can choose the old regime while filing ITR if it gives you more tax savings [citation:3][citation:8].
