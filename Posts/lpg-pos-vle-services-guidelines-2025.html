POS/VLE Services for LPG Distribution: Complete Guidelines & MoU Terms

Memorandum of Understanding — Operational Guidelines for CSC Village Level Entrepreneurs under IOCL, BPCL & HPCL

Sarat Rout April 18, 2025 10 min read LPG Services
CSC Village Level Entrepreneurs bridging the last-mile gap in LPG distribution across rural India

To address the shortage of LPG distribution centres in remote and rural areas, Oil Marketing Companies (OMCs) — Indian Oil Corporation Limited (IOCL), Bharat Petroleum Corporation Limited (BPCL), and Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited (HPCL) — signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with CSC e-Governance Services India Limited (CSC SPV) under the Digital India programme. Under this MoU, Common Service Centres (CSCs) — operated by Village Level Entrepreneurs (VLEs) — are authorised to serve as Points of Sale (POS) for LPG services, bringing essential cooking gas connections and refill booking services closer to the doorstep of beneficiaries. This article details the complete operational guidelines governing POS/VLE services under the Sub-MoU between the OMC LPG Distributor and the POS/VLE.

1. Background — OMC-CSC MoU and Digital India

  Sub-MoU — Operational Guidelines

POS/VLE Services for LPG Distribution

Executed between the OMC LPG Distributor and the POS/VLE (CSC Centre), under the Main MoU signed between IOCL, BPCL, HPCL, and CSC e-Governance Services India Limited (CSC SPV). This Sub-MoU becomes Null & Void automatically upon termination of the POS/VLE as per Clause 1.1(d) of the Main MoU.

IOCL BPCL HPCL CSC SPV Digital Seva Portal

The MoU was signed in the presence of the Minister of Law & Justice and Electronics & Information Technology (MeitY) and the Minister of Petroleum & Natural Gas. As on date, over 3.15 lakh CSCs are functional across India — of which 2.10 lakh are at the Gram Panchayat level — and 60,000 are operated by women VLEs. The inclusion of LPG distribution services through CSCs addresses the critical last-mile delivery gap, particularly in rural, remote, and tribal areas where formal LPG distributorships may not be viable.

2. Scope of Services by POS/VLE

Under this Sub-MoU, the OMC LPG Distributor shall facilitate the following services through the POS/VLE to its registered LPG customers:

Booking New LPG Connections

The VLE is authorised to facilitate applications and bookings for new domestic LPG connections under:

  • Ujjwala (PMUY) category — for BPL and economically weaker households
  • General category — for all other eligible customers

LPG Refill Booking

Booking of LPG refills for 14.2 kg domestic cylinders on behalf of registered customers of the mapped LPG Distributor. This service is conducted through the Digital Seva Portal.

Supply and Distribution of Cylinders

Physical supply and distribution of LPG cylinders to customers — with the VLE premises serving as a satellite distribution point for storage up to 100 kg of LPG in cylinders.

3. Functional and Operational Modalities

The Sub-MoU establishes specific functional modalities governing the relationship between the OMC LPG Distributor and the POS/VLE. These ensure safe, compliant, and transparent operation of LPG services at the VLE premises.

4. PESO-Compliant Cylinder Storage Requirements

Cylinder Storage — Safety & PESO Compliance

100 kg

Maximum LPG Storage at VLE Premises at Any Given Time

  1. The POS/VLE shall make provision of a Cylinder Storage Cage, designed and built as per the design approved by the Petroleum and Explosives Safety Organisation (PESO), for safe and secure storage of LPG cylinders at its premises.
  2. The POS/VLE shall not store more than 100 kg of LPG in cylinders at any given time — approximately seven 14.2 kg cylinders.
  3. An initial stock of around seven 14.2 kg cylinders will be supplied by the Distributor and will be accounted as inventory at the Distributor's go-down.
  4. The cage must be installed in a well-ventilated, fire-safe location at the VLE premises, away from ignition sources, as per PESO guidelines under the Gas Cylinder Rules, 2016.
CSC Bears Cage Cost: The one-time cost of making the PESO-approved caged storage for 100 kg LPG at the POS/VLE premises will be borne entirely by CSC SPV — not the VLE or the Distributor.

5. Equipment, Training, and Pre-Delivery Checks

Pre-Delivery Check Equipment

The Distributor shall provide the POS/VLE with the following equipment on a returnable basis:

  • Weighing scale (for net weight verification)
  • O-ring tester (for valve seal integrity)
  • Leak detector (for gas leak safety check)
These must be used to conduct mandatory pre-delivery checks on every cylinder before it is handed over to a customer.

Training for VLE and Staff

The Distributor is responsible for providing training to the POS/VLE and its staff on:

  • Safe handling and storage of LPG cylinders
  • Pre-delivery safety inspection procedures
  • Use of the Digital Seva Portal for bookings
  • Customer documentation and record-keeping

Pre-Delivery Safety Protocol

Before delivering any cylinder to a customer, the VLE must:

  • Verify net weight against the declared weight on the seal
  • Test the O-ring for proper sealing
  • Check for gas leaks using the leak detector
  • Record the delivery details in the register

6. Payment Settlement Structure

The following payment structure applies for services rendered by the POS/VLE under this Sub-MoU. All payments from the Distributor to the VLE are made through CSC SPV as per the agreed schedule.

S.No. Service Type Charges Mode of Payment
1 New LPG connection booking (PMUY / Ujjwala & General category) Per transaction — as per CSC SPV guidelines Paid by CSC SPV to VLE as per agreed commission structure
2 LPG refill booking Per transaction — as per Distributor-VLE arrangement Paid by Distributor to VLE through CSC SPV
3 Cylinder distribution — delivered by Distributor to VLE premises ₹10 per cylinder Paid by Distributor to VLE on delivery of cylinders at VLE premises
4 Cylinder distribution — VLE uplifts from Distributor's go-down using own vehicle ₹19.50 per cylinder Paid by Distributor to VLE on delivery of cylinders at go-down
Payment Logic: The higher rate of ₹19.50 per cylinder applies when the VLE uses its own transport to collect cylinders from the Distributor's go-down — compensating for additional logistics cost. When the Distributor delivers directly to the VLE premises, the charge is ₹10 per cylinder. Both rates cover only the last-mile distribution service rendered by the VLE.

7. Regulatory Compliance and VLE Obligations

No Payments to Distributor or Third Parties

The VLE shall not pay any amount to the Distributor or to any third party as a condition of obtaining or retaining POS/VLE services. Any such demand by a Distributor constitutes a violation of the MoU.

Registration Deposit — ₹1,000 Only

The VLE shall pay a refundable interest-free security deposit of ₹1,000 to CSC SPV via the Digital Seva Portal. This is the only payment the VLE makes. No other fee, deposit, or charge is payable to anyone else.

MoU Documentation

A certified copy of the Sub-MoU must be submitted to the respective OMC and CSC SPV for record. The VLE must retain a copy for compliance purposes.

Record-Keeping and MDG Compliance

The VLE is required to comply with applicable Marketing Discipline Guidelines (MDGs) of the OMC. These govern proper record-keeping, customer service, safe cylinder handling, and anti-hoarding compliance.

8. Penalties, Liabilities, and Termination

Penalties and Liability Provisions

  • If any irregularity (as defined in the Marketing Discipline Guidelines applicable to OMC LPG distributors) is established at any POS/VLE, the respective OMC shall take punitive action against CSC SPV — including monetary penalty or outright termination of that particular POS/VLE — as per the Main MoU terms.
  • However, no punitive action shall be admissible against the OMC LPG Distributor unless direct involvement of the Distributor in the irregularity is established during investigation by the inspecting authority.
  • CSC SPV is responsible for reimbursing any equipment loss or damage to the OMC within 15 days of a formal claim being lodged.
  • In case of shortage of LPG cylinders at VLE premises, the VLE shall reimburse the Distributor at ₹2,300 per 14.2 kg cylinder within 7 days (one week) from the date of the Distributor's claim.
  • If the VLE fails to make the ₹2,300 per cylinder payment, the matter shall be referred to the State SPOC (as listed in Clause 8 of the Sub-MoU), and CSC SPV will then ensure payment is made to the respective OMC Distributor.
  • If payment is not resolved even then, the OMC shall take up the matter directly with CSC SPV for settlement.
  1. Shortage Identified: Distributor lodges a formal shortage claim against the VLE.
  2. VLE Payment Window: VLE must pay ₹2,300/cylinder within 7 days of the claim date.
  3. SPOC Escalation: If VLE fails to pay, the State SPOC (CSC SPV or OMC) is notified and CSC ensures payment.
  4. OMC-CSC SPV Resolution: If still unresolved, OMC takes up the matter directly with CSC SPV for final settlement.
  5. Punitive Action: Persistent non-compliance or established irregularity leads to termination of VLE/POS services and monetary recovery.

9. Grievance Redressal — Single Points of Contact (SPOCs)

Both parties — the Distributor and the POS/VLE — may report their issues and grievances to the designated Single Points of Contact (SPOCs) for their respective organisations at the State level. The SPOC system ensures timely resolution without requiring escalation to national-level offices for routine disputes.

10. Dispute Resolution

Final and Binding Mechanism

Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Sub-MoU — whether between the Distributor and the VLE, or between either party and CSC SPV — shall be reported to the concerned OMC and CSC SPV. The decision of the OMC and CSC SPV, arrived at after considering the representations of all parties, shall be final and binding on the Distributor and the POS/VLE. This agreement operates with the mutual consent of both parties.

The POS/VLE model is a win-win under Digital India — VLEs earn from LPG service delivery, rural customers get doorstep access, and OMCs extend their distribution reach to the last mile through CSCs.

In summary: The Sub-MoU between the OMC LPG Distributor and the POS/VLE (CSC centre) is a carefully designed operational framework that defines clear obligations, payments, safety norms, and accountability at every level. VLEs serve as trusted last-mile agents — extending LPG booking, cylinder distribution, and new connection services to rural and semi-urban beneficiaries who would otherwise face significant access barriers. Strict compliance with PESO storage norms, the MDG-based discipline framework, and the refundable ₹1,000 deposit (to CSC SPV only) are the cornerstones of a compliant and profitable POS/VLE operation.
