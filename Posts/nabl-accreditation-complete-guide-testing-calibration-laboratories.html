The National Accreditation Board for Testing and Calibration Laboratories (NABL)

Complete Guide to Accreditation Scope, ISO Standards, International Recognition & 2024–25 Updates

Sarat Rout February 17, 2025 12 min read Laboratory Accreditation
NABL-accredited laboratories meet global standards for testing and calibration across science and industry

The National Accreditation Board for Testing and Calibration Laboratories (NABL) is India's premier laboratory accreditation body — an autonomous constituent board of the Quality Council of India (QCI), operating under the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India. As the sole authorized accreditation body for testing and calibration laboratories in India, NABL provides third-party assessment of technical competence against internationally recognised standards, enabling Indian laboratories to gain global recognition for their test and calibration results.

1. What is NABL? — Overview and Governance

National Accreditation Board for Testing and Calibration Laboratories

NABL provides voluntary, non-discriminatory accreditation services to Conformity Assessment Bodies (CABs) — including testing laboratories, calibration laboratories, medical testing laboratories, proficiency testing providers (PTPs), reference material producers (RMPs), and biobanks — in India and abroad, regardless of ownership, size, or group membership.

NABL's accreditation system is established in accordance with ISO/IEC 17011 — the international standard governing the requirements for accreditation bodies themselves. This ensures that NABL's own processes are as rigorous as those it assesses.

NABL was established with the objective of providing the Government, industry associations, and industry at large with a robust scheme for third-party assessment of laboratory quality and technical competence. Accreditation by NABL assures stakeholders that a laboratory consistently produces accurate, reliable, and traceable test or calibration results — not just at the time of assessment, but on an ongoing basis through regular surveillance.

2. Accreditation Scope — Types of Conformity Assessment Bodies

NABL accredits six distinct categories of Conformity Assessment Bodies (CABs). Multi-disciplinary CABs must apply separately for each discipline, ensuring that the accreditation reflects actual, verified competence in each field.

ISO/IEC 17025

Testing Laboratories

Covers biological, chemical, electrical, electronics, mechanical, fluid-flow, non-destructive testing, photometry, radiological, thermal, and forensics disciplines.

ISO/IEC 17025

Calibration Laboratories

Covers electro-technical, mechanical, fluid flow, thermal, optical, and radiological calibration. Ensures measurement traceability to national and international standards.

ISO 15189 : 2022

Medical Testing Laboratories

Clinical Biochemistry, Clinical Pathology, Haematology, Microbiology, Histopathology, Cytopathology, Genetics, and Nuclear Medicine (in-vitro). Transition to ISO 15189:2022 completed by December 2025.

ISO/IEC 17043 : 2023

Proficiency Testing Providers

Conformity assessment for proficiency testing (PT) schemes. PTPs are critical for verifying that laboratories perform reliably across different measurement conditions. Standard transitioned to 2023 version.

ISO 17034 : 2016

Reference Material Producers

General requirements for the competence of bodies producing certified and non-certified reference materials used to verify calibration and test method accuracy.

ISO 20387

Biobanks

A new category introduced in 2024 (NABL 229). Covers biobanking activities including biological material categories, storage conditions, and associated methods. Supports India's biotechnology and research ecosystem.

NABL is a full signatory member to Mutual Recognition Arrangements (MRAs) with the International Laboratory Accreditation Cooperation (ILAC) and the Asia Pacific Accreditation Cooperation (APAC) for the accreditation of Testing and Calibration Laboratories (ISO/IEC 17025), Medical Testing Laboratories (ISO 15189), Proficiency Testing Providers (ISO/IEC 17043), and Reference Material Producers (ISO 17034).

As of 1 January 2026, a new unified global accreditation body — the Global Accreditation Cooperation Incorporated (GAC) — has been established, bringing together the work of ILAC and the International Accreditation Forum (IAF). NABL's international recognition transitions to this new framework.

Practical benefit: Test and calibration results from NABL-accredited laboratories are accepted by MRA partner countries without the need for re-testing — significantly reducing trade barriers, saving time and cost for exporters, and enhancing India's position in international commerce.

6. The Accreditation Process — Step by Step

NABL accreditation follows a structured process governed by NABL 100B — Accreditation Process & Procedure. The entire process, from application to certificate, is conducted through the NABL online portal. No hard copy applications are accepted.

  1. Gap Assessment: The laboratory reviews the applicable standard (ISO/IEC 17025, ISO 15189, etc.) and conducts an internal gap analysis to identify non-conformities with NABL accreditation criteria.
  2. Quality System Documentation: The laboratory prepares its Quality Manual, procedures, work instructions, and records in accordance with the standard and NABL-specific guidance documents (e.g., NABL 160A for Testing/Calibration labs).
  3. Online Application: Application is submitted through the NABL web portal with supporting documents, defined scope of accreditation, and applicable fees. NABL assigns a unique laboratory ID upon successful submission.
  4. Document Review: NABL reviews the Quality Manual, procedures, and technical records for compliance. Non-conformities at this stage must be addressed before assessment.
  5. Pre-Assessment Visit (Optional): A preliminary visit by NABL assessors evaluates readiness and provides actionable feedback. Particularly useful for laboratories applying for the first time.
  6. On-Site Assessment: A team of qualified NABL assessors visits the laboratory to inspect facilities, equipment calibration, staff competence, environmental conditions, and actual test/calibration procedures against the applied scope.
  7. Proficiency Testing (PT): The laboratory must have satisfactorily participated in at least one recognised Proficiency Testing programme covering its major scope areas. Ongoing PT participation is required post-accreditation.
  8. Corrective Actions: Non-conformities identified during assessment must be addressed with evidence of root cause analysis and corrective action within the stipulated time.
  9. Accreditation Grant: Upon closure of all non-conformities, the Accreditation Committee grants accreditation. The certificate is made available on the NABL portal after all dues are cleared.
  10. Surveillance and Re-assessment: Annual on-site surveillance is conducted, with reassessment every 2 years. The certificate is valid for 4 years (enhanced from 2 years effective October 2024).
2024 Update: NABL extended accreditation validity from 2 years to 4 years, with yearly on-site surveillance and reassessment every 2 years. This reduces administrative burden on laboratories while maintaining rigorous ongoing oversight.

7. Quality Assurance and Continuous Improvement

Continuous Compliance through Surveillance

Accredited laboratories are subject to regular on-site annual surveillance visits and reassessment every two years. This ensures that they continue to comply with accreditation criteria — not just at the time of initial assessment. NABL assessors evaluate equipment calibration status, participation in PT programmes, personnel training records, management review minutes, and adherence to corrective action commitments.

The accreditation framework mandates that laboratories maintain a complete Measurement Uncertainty (MU) budget for each parameter in their scope. For calibration laboratories in particular, demonstrating calibration and measurement capability (CMC) with full traceability to national or international measurement standards is a fundamental requirement.

NABL also mandates regular internal audits and management reviews — structural quality management tools that compel laboratories to identify weaknesses, implement improvements, and document evidence of learning and progress. This culture of self-improvement is one of the most lasting benefits of accreditation.

NABL's Gunvatta Pathshala Program: NABL runs dedicated training programs (Gunvatta Pathshala) for laboratory personnel on ISO standards including ISO 17034:2016, ISO/IEC 17043:2023, and ISO 13528:2022. It also conducts Gunvatta Yatra awareness programs for stakeholders. These initiatives strengthen the overall quality ecosystem, not just individual accredited laboratories.

8. Regulatory Integration — FSSAI, CDSCO, CPCB and Others

NABL's most significant operational advantage for laboratories is its system of Integrated Assessments. Rather than requiring laboratories to undergo separate assessments — first for NABL accreditation and then for regulatory recognition — integrated assessments combine both processes into a single visit. This reduces time, cost, and administrative complexity significantly.

Regulators that currently conduct Integrated Assessments with NABL include:

Legal Requirement Across Sectors: In India, NABL accreditation is a statutory or regulatory prerequisite for laboratories in several sectors. Under FSSAI, food testing laboratories must be NABL-accredited. CDSCO requires drug testing laboratories to hold accreditation. CPCB mandates NABL accreditation for environmental testing (water, air, and soil analysis). Without accreditation, test reports from such laboratories may not be legally accepted by regulatory authorities.

10. Recent Updates — 2024 and 2025

October 2024 — Accreditation Validity Extended to 4 Years: NABL extended accreditation validity from 2 years to 4 years, with annual on-site surveillance and reassessment at the 2-year mark. This reduces the administrative cycle for laboratories while maintaining rigorous oversight.
September 2024 — Biobank Accreditation Introduced (NABL 229): NABL released specific criteria for the accreditation of Biobanks under ISO 20387 — covering categories of biological material, storage conditions, and associated biobanking activities. Amended in April 2025 to reflect updated requirements.
December 2025 — ISO 15189:2022 Transition Completed: All medical testing laboratories were required to transition from ISO 15189:2012 to ISO 15189:2022 by 31 December 2025. As of January 2026, NABL accepts applications only under the 2022 version.
June 2023 — PTP Standard Update: Proficiency Testing Providers transitioned from ISO/IEC 17043:2010 to ISO/IEC 17043:2023. Training programs under Gunvatta Pathshala support laboratories through this transition.
January 2026 — Global Accreditation Cooperation (GAC) Established: The International Laboratory Accreditation Cooperation (ILAC) and the International Accreditation Forum (IAF) merged into a new unified body — the Global Accreditation Cooperation Incorporated (GAC). NABL's international MRA recognition now transitions to this global framework.

11. Conclusion

NABL Accreditation — A Hallmark of Quality and Competence

NABL accreditation is the definitive standard of excellence for testing and calibration laboratories in India. It is not merely a compliance exercise — it is a comprehensive framework for building and sustaining technical credibility, operational excellence, and global recognition.

For laboratories seeking to demonstrate their commitment to quality and gain international acceptance, NABL accreditation provides:

  • International recognition through ILAC, APAC, and now the new GAC MRA framework
  • Technical credibility through rigorous third-party assessment against global ISO standards
  • Expanded validity of 4 years with annual surveillance (updated October 2024)
  • Regulatory integration with FSSAI, CDSCO, CPCB, EIC, APEDA, and others
  • Continuous improvement through structured PT participation and surveillance cycles
  • Market access for exports, government tenders, and product certifications
  • Risk mitigation through proactive quality management and uncertainty estimation
  • Sector expansion into Biobanks (ISO 20387) and medical imaging (NABL 135)

For any laboratory in India — from a food testing unit to a forensic lab, from a medical diagnostic centre to a biobank — NABL accreditation is the surest path to establishing and sustaining global-standard scientific credibility.
