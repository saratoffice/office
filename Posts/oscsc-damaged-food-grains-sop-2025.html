Standard Operating Procedure for Disposal of Damaged Food Grains

The document outlines a Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for the disposal of damaged food grains by the Odisha State Civil Supplies Corporation (OSCSC) Ltd. This procedure is in place to handle cases where food grains, especially rice and wheat, have become damaged during storage and are deemed unfit for human consumption. The SOP aims to establish clear guidelines for identification, categorization, and disposal of such food grains, ensuring that these damaged stocks are disposed of efficiently and in compliance with relevant regulations. Below is a summary of the key points covered in the SOP:

üîç 2. Verification and Disposal Process

The verification and disposal of damaged food grains involve two levels of committees:

District Level Technical Committee (DLTC)

  • Carries out initial verification of damaged stock
  • Conducts sample analysis
  • Performs categorization of damaged grains

State Level Joint Verification Committee (SLIVC)

  • Performs random checks of DLTC's categorization
  • 20% of samples are verified
  • Ensures accuracy of categorization
Identification
DLTC Verification
SLIVC Random Check
Disposal

Timeline: The entire process from identification to disposal should be completed within four months.

If significant damage is identified, an inquiry will be conducted to determine the cause, and action will be taken against responsible parties.

üí∞ 3. Sale of Damaged Food Grains

Damaged food grains are to be disposed of through tender/auction to bona fide users, including manufacturers, consumers, or State PSU entities that require such stocks for non-human consumption purposes.

The reserve price for tender sales is based on a percentage of the Minimum Support Price (MSP) for each crop year:

Category Percentage of MSP Typical Use
Feed-I 70-80% of MSP Poultry feed, animal feed
Feed-II 60-70% of MSP Animal feed
Feed-III 50-60% of MSP Lower grade animal feed
Industrial Use 40-50% of MSP Ethanol production, starch industry
Manure Use 10-20% of MSP Composting, fertilizer
Note: MSP (Minimum Support Price) is announced by Government of India each crop year. Actual reserve price is determined based on category and prevailing market conditions.

üå± 4. Disposal of Stocks Categorized as "Manure Use"

Special Procedure for Manure Category

Stocks categorized as "Manure Use" may not have tender bidders. In such cases:

  1. Stocks are offered to local municipal, Panchayat, or corporation authorities free of cost
  2. A certificate must be obtained confirming proper disposal
  3. If no response is received, the stocks are to be dumped under official supervision
  4. Records of the disposal are to be maintained for audit purposes
Manure category grains may be used for composting

üåê 5. Information Dissemination

The OSCSC is responsible for ensuring transparency in the disposal process. The OSCSC website must be regularly updated with information related to damaged food grains, including:

  • Categorization details
  • Quantity of damaged stock
  • Tender/auction notices
  • Disposal records

üìã 6. Delegation of Powers for Disposal

The process for disposal is subject to delegation based on the quantity of the damaged stock:

Quantity Competent Authority
Up to 5 MT Collector of the district
More than 5 MT up to 50 MT Managing Director, OSCSC
More than 50 MT up to 100 MT Board of Directors, OSCSC
More than 100 MT up to 200 MT Secretary to Govt., FS & CW Department
Above 200 MT Govt. in FS & CW Department

SOP Objectives

This SOP ensures that food grains damaged during storage are dealt with swiftly and in an organized manner, preventing further deterioration in quality, and facilitating timely disposal. The guidelines emphasize transparency and accountability, ensuring that all procedures are carried out efficiently and in compliance with relevant regulations.
