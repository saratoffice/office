‚ùì Answering Common Questions

Q: Difference between CNG and PNG?

CNG (Compressed Natural Gas) is stored at high pressure and is primarily used as a vehicle fuel.

PNG (Piped Natural Gas) is delivered through pipelines at lower pressures and is mainly used for residential cooking, heating, and industrial purposes.

Q: Difference between LNG and NGL?

LNG (Liquefied Natural Gas) is natural gas cooled to a liquid state (-162¬∞C) for easy transportation and storage. It is primarily composed of methane.

NGL (Natural Gas Liquids) are a mixture of liquid hydrocarbons like propane, butane, and others that are separated from natural gas during processing.

Q: Is PNG vapour heavier or lighter than air?

PNG vapour is lighter than air, similar to natural gas itself, which is primarily methane.

Q: Is the PNG leak detector installed at kitchen roof ceiling level correct?

NO, this is INCORRECT!

PNG (which is methane) is lighter than air, so the leak detector should be placed near the floor where any leaking gas would accumulate. Installing it at ceiling level would delay detection of a leak.

Q: Can LNG be stored in a Horton sphere tank?

NO, LNG is a cryogenic liquid and requires cryogenic storage tanks that can handle extremely low temperatures (-162¬∞C), not a standard Horton sphere tank.

Summary

The key differences between petroleum gases lie in their:

  • Chemical Composition: LPG is propane/butane; others are primarily methane
  • Physical State: LPG/LNG/NGL are liquids (different temperatures); CNG/PNG are gases
  • Storage Pressure: From atmospheric (NGL) to very high pressure (CNG)
  • Density relative to air: Critical for safety ‚Äì LPG sinks, methane-based gases rise
  • Applications: Cooking (LPG/PNG), vehicles (CNG/LNG), industry (all), petrochemicals (NGL)

Understanding these differences is essential for safe handling, proper storage, and appropriate application selection.
