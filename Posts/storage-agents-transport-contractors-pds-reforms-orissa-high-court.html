S.K. Sahoo, J

This matter is taken up by video conferencing mode. Learned counsel for the petitioner submitted that the case has already been committed to the Court of Session and now pending in the Court of learned Sessions Judge, Nayagarh in S.T. Case No.28 of 2021.

Heard learned counsel for the petitioner and learned counsel for the State.

This is an application under section 439 of Cr.P.C. for grant of bail to the petitioner in connection with Odagaon P.S. Case No. 203 of 2020 corresponding to S.T. Case No. 28 of 2021 pending in the Court of learned Sessions Judge, Nayagarh for offences punishable under sections 498-A/302/304-B/34 of the Indian Penal Code read with section 4 of the D.P. Act.

The petitioner moved an application for bail before the learned Additional Sessions Judge, Nayagarh, which was rejected on 04.02.2021.

Learned counsel for the petitioner submitted that the petitioner is the paternal uncle-in-law of the deceased Jhumuri Sahoo and he is in judicial custody since 23.10.2020 and he has been charge sheeted under sections 498-A/302/304-B/34 of the Indian Penal Code read with section 4 of D.P. Act. It is further contended that the marriage of the deceased was solemnized on 17.02.2014 and she committed suicide on 23.10.2020 and as per the post mortem report, the cause of death of the deceased was combined effect of venous congestion and asphyxia and no other external injury except the ligature mark around the neck was noticed on the person of the deceased. He further submitted that there is absolutely no material on record to make out a case under section 302 of the Indian Penal Code and the petitioner is staying in separate mess and the allegations against the petitioner are omnibus in nature and therefore, the bail application of the petitioner may be favourably considered.

Learned counsel for the State produced the case diary and opposed the prayer for bail.

Court's Decision: Considering the submissions made by the learned counsel for the respective parties, the nature of accusation against the petitioner, the relationship between the petitioner and the deceased, the absence of any clinching material against the petitioner, the post mortem report findings and further taking into account the period of detention of the petitioner in judicial custody, I am inclined to release the petitioner on bail.

Let the petitioner be released on bail in the aforesaid case on furnishing a bail bond of Rs.50,000/- (rupees fifty thousand) with two local solvent sureties each for the like amount to the satisfaction of the learned Court in seisin over the matter with further conditions as the learned Court may deem just and proper.

The BLAPL is accordingly disposed of.

As the restrictions due to resurgence of COVID-19 situation are continuing, learned counsel for the parties may utilize a printout of the order available in the High Court's website, at par with certified copy, subject to attestation by the concerned advocate, in the manner prescribed vide Court's Notice No. 4587 dated 25th March 2020 as modified by Court's Notice No. 4798 dated 15th April 2021.

📜 Background: Justice Wadhwa Committee Recommendations

12.07.2006
Order passed in WP (C) No. 196 of 2007 of Supreme Court constituting committee headed by Justice D.P. Wadhwa as Chairman of the Central Vigilance Committee on Public Distribution System.
10.11.2008
Committee submitted report and recommendations to Hon'ble Supreme Court for smooth distribution of PDS commodities. Recommended abolition of private storage agency and not to appoint Private Storage Agents after expiry of their extended terms.
26.11.2011
Government communicated decision to operationalize departmental storage centres in place of private storage agents and to deliver PDS stock at retail points.
14.12.2011
Advertisement published in Odia Newspaper "The Sambad" inviting tender for appointment of contractors for handling and transportation of food grains.

Supreme Court Direction: Pursuant to the order of Hon'ble Supreme Court dated 10.11.2008, the Wadhwa Committee's report and recommendations were accepted. The Committee classified the maladies affecting PDS in Odisha and in Chapter-IX recommended for smooth distribution of PDS commodities. The State of Odisha constituted an Apex Committee headed by the Chief Secretary to implement the recommendations.

📝 The Writ Petitions

W.P(C) No. 5689 of 2012

Petitioner: Storage Agent for Cuttack Sadar Block appointed on 10.11.2008

Prayer: To quash Annexure-2 and the decision for appointment of Transport Contractor, and to allow petitioner to continue as storage agent by extending licence issued under Annexure-1.

Grounds: The tender notice would adversely affect the Distribution System, hike costs, and affect rights of petitioners.

W.P(C) No. 1309 of 2012

Petitioners: Licensees to operate as wholesaler/storage agents of rice, wheat, sugar under PDS

Period of validity: Extended till 31st March, 2012

Prayer: To extend validity of their licences and challenge advertisement dated 14.12.2011 (Annexure-4)

W.P(C) No. 1310 of 2012

Petitioners: Licensees to operate as wholesaler/storage agents under PDS

Period of validity: Extended till 31st March, 2012

Prayer: To extend validity of licences and challenge advertisement dated 14.12.2011 (Annexure-4)

Petitioners' Grievances

  • Notice under Annexure-4 is against public interest and rights of petitioners along with 766 storage agents throughout the State.
  • About 80 thousand people directly and indirectly depend on this process/work.
  • Tender notice will result in rise of cost of transportation, leading to hike in prices of Control Commodities.
  • Petitioners have invested security deposit to carry on business.
  • The decision is contrary to Wadhwa Committee recommendations which suggested departmental operation with requisite manpower and equipment.
  • Without implementing Wadhwa Committee recommendations, opposite parties directed Collectors to appoint contractors in place of private storage agents.

🏛️ Government's Stand

Mr. Nanda, learned counsel for opposite party No. 3: Submitted that there is no illegality in floating notice inviting tender. The writ petition is devoid of merit.

Mr. Banamali Sahoo, learned counsel for opposite party No. 4 (OSCSC Ltd.):

  • Corporation is fully owned by State Government under administrative control of Food Supplies and Consumer Welfare Department.
  • Hon'ble Supreme Court on basis of Wadhwa Commission report ordered Government to ensure doorstep delivery of food grains to ration shops.
  • Government in Food, Supplies and Consumer Welfare Department communicated decision to operationalize departmental storage centres in place of private storage agents.
  • Policy already implemented in four districts (Sonepur, Deogarh, Nuapada, Boudh) since November 2011 and from 01.04.2012 in rest of districts.
  • Requisite manpower and equipments are provided – Procurement Inspectors posted, RRC-cum-DSC equipped with weighing scale, moisture meter, etc.
  • Petitioner in W.P(C) No. 5689/2012 continued till 31.03.2012 but did not submit tender for appointment as Handling and Transport Contractor.

📄 Government Letter No. 20353 dated 18.11.2011

Relevant extract:

"Sir,

In inviting reference to the letter on the subject cited above I am to say that Govt. in FS & CW Deptt. in their letter No. 11328 dt.01.07.2011 has communicated decision of the Govt. to operationalise the Departmental Storage Centre in place of Private Storage Agents and to deliver PDS stock at Retail points.

2. The Govt. in FS & CW Deptt. has extended tenure of Private Storage Agents till 31.03.2012 which has been communicated to districts in this office letter No. 16980 dt.19.09.2011

3. As such, Departmental operation in place of Private Storage Agents and door delivery of PDS stock to retail points will start from 1 April, 2012 in respect of all Blocks/ULBs from the concerned RRC-cum-DSC."

❓ Questions for Determination

(i) Whether the decision taken by OPS for appointment of transport contractor for handling and transport of food grains and the tender call notice No. 1838 dated 28.11.2011 suffers from any illegality or infirmity so as to be liable to be quashed?

(ii) Whether in the facts and circumstances of the case, relief claimed by the petitioners can be granted?

⚖️ Legal Principles on Policy Decisions

Tamil Nadu Education Deptt. v. State of Tamil Nadu, AIR 1980 SC 379:

"Once the principle is found to be rational, the fact that a few freak instances of hardship may arise on either side cannot be ground to invalidate the order or the policy."

Sterling Computers Ltd. v. M & N Publications Ltd., AIR 1996 SC 51:

"If the decisions have been taken in bona fide manner although not strictly following the norms laid down by the Courts, such decisions are upheld... Courts while judging the constitutional validity of executive decisions must grant certain measure of freedom of play in the joints to the executive."

Ugar Sugar Works Ltd. v. Delhi Administration, (2001) 3 SCC 635:

"Courts do not ordinarily interfere with the policy decisions of the executive unless the policy can be faulted on the ground of mala fide, unreasonableness, arbitrariness or unfairness."

Krishnan Kakkanth v. Govt. of Kerala, AIR 1997 SC 128:

"Unless the policy decision is demonstrably capricious or arbitrary and not informed by any reason whatsoever or it suffers from the vice of discrimination or infringes any statute or provisions of the Constitution, the policy decision cannot be struck down."

🔍 Court's Findings and Analysis

  1. Undisputedly, the petitioner in W.P(C) No. 5689 of 2012 was a Storage agent of Cuttack block and continued till 31.03.2012. He did not submit tender for appointment as Handling and Transport Contractor.
  2. The Corporation is fully owned by State Government and implements Government decisions.
  3. Hon'ble Supreme Court on basis of Wadhwa Commission report ordered Government to ensure doorstep delivery of food grains to ration shops.
  4. Commissioner-cum-Secretary vide letter No. 366 dated 06.01.2011 communicated that as per Wadhwa Commission recommendation, private storage agency system is required to be abolished.
  5. Apex Committee recommended abolition of private storage agency in compliance with Wadhwa recommendations.
  6. Government decided not to appoint private storage agents in the State after expiry of their extended term.
  7. Managing Director of Corporation vide letter No. 20353 dated 18.11.2011 directed Collectors regarding functioning of departmental storage centres and lifting of stock from RRC-cum-DSC to retail points.

Therefore, Annexure-2 has been issued pursuant to the policy decision of the Government in Food, Supplies and Consumer Welfare Department.

Court's Conclusion on Policy Decision

It is settled legal proposition that policy decision taken by the State Government or its authorities/instrumentalities is beyond the purview of judicial review unless the same is found to be arbitrary, unreasonable or in contravention of the statutory provisions or violates the rights of individual guaranteed under the Statute.

Undisputedly, the State Government pursuant to report/recommendation of Justice Wadhwa Commission appointed by the Hon'ble Supreme Court has taken a policy decision not to appoint private storage agent in the State and to operationalize the departmental storage centres in place of private storage agent and to deliver PDS stock at retail point for smooth distribution of PDS stock.

We do not find any fault in the said policy on the point of unreasonableness, arbitrariness, unfairness to render the policy irrational or unconstitutional.

📋 Final Judgment

Writ Petitions DISMISSED

  • W.P(C) No. 5689 of 2012: Petitioner appointed as Storage Agent in 2008-09, extended till March 2012. Licence granted for specific period. After expiry, no right for renewal when policy decision taken by Government.
  • W.P(C) No. 1309 & 1310 of 2012: No illegality in inviting tender under Annexure-4 for engagement of contractor/transporter. Period of validity of licence cannot be extended beyond 31 March 2012.
  • All writ petitions are devoid of merit and accordingly dismissed.

📌 Key Legal Principles Established

Principle Holding
Judicial Review of Policy Decisions Courts do not interfere with policy decisions unless arbitrary, unreasonable, mala fide, or unconstitutional.
Rational Principle Once principle is rational, hardship to some cannot invalidate policy.
Government's Bona Fide Decisions Courts grant freedom to executive in bona fide decisions even if norms not strictly followed.
Licence Renewal No right to renewal after expiry when policy decision changes.
Implementation of Supreme Court Directives Government bound to implement recommendations accepted by Supreme Court.

📝 Summary & Takeaways

  • Wadhwa Committee Recommendations: Supreme Court accepted recommendations to abolish private storage agency system and ensure doorstep delivery of food grains.
  • Government Policy Decision: State Government decided to replace private storage agents with departmental storage centres and transport contractors.
  • Policy Upheld: Court found policy rational, not arbitrary or unreasonable, and therefore beyond judicial review.
  • No Right to Renewal: Storage agents whose licences expired on 31.03.2012 had no right to continue after policy change.
  • Implementation: Government successfully implemented doorstep delivery system through Handling and Transport Contractors in all districts.

This judgment reaffirms that policy decisions taken by government pursuant to Supreme Court directives, aimed at improving public distribution systems, will not be interfered with by courts merely because they affect private business interests. The focus remains on public welfare and efficient delivery of essential commodities to citizens.
