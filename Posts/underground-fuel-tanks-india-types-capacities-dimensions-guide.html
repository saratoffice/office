Underground Storage Tanks (USTs) at Indian retail outlets – MS & HSD storage

Underground Fuel Tanks Used in India

Underground Storage Tanks (USTs) used at fuel retail outlets in India are designed and installed as per safety norms under the Petroleum Rules, 2002 and safety guidelines issued by the Petroleum and Explosives Safety Organisation (PESO).

📦 Types of Underground Fuel Tanks

1️⃣ Mild Steel (MS) Underground Tanks

MS Horizontal Tank
MS Tank Installation
Epoxy Coated MS Tank
RCC Pit Installation

🔹 Description:

Traditional horizontal cylindrical steel tanks, installed below ground with anti-corrosion coating.

🔹 Features:

  • Fabricated from mild steel plates
  • Epoxy / coal tar coating for corrosion resistance
  • Installed in RCC pit with sand cushioning
  • Cathodic protection (sometimes provided)

🔹 Advantages:

  • Strong & widely used
  • Lower initial cost

🔹 Limitations:

  • Susceptible to corrosion
  • Requires periodic inspection
  • Risk of soil contamination if leakage occurs

📌 Commonly used in older retail outlets.

2️⃣ Double-Wall Steel Tanks

Double-Wall Steel Tank
Interstitial Space Monitoring
FRP Jacketed Steel Tank
Modern Double-Wall Design

🔹 Description:

Two steel walls with an interstitial space between them for leak monitoring.

🔹 Features:

  • Inner tank stores fuel
  • Outer tank acts as secondary containment
  • Leak detection sensors installed

🔹 Advantages:

  • Early leak detection
  • Higher environmental safety
  • Increasingly adopted at modern outlets

3️⃣ Fiberglass Reinforced Plastic (FRP) Tanks

FRP Underground Tanks
FRP Tank Installation
FRP Storage Tank
FRP Tank Manufacturing

🔹 Description:

Non-metallic composite tanks made of fiberglass reinforced plastic.

🔹 Features:

  • Corrosion resistant
  • Lightweight compared to steel
  • Often double-wall design
  • Compatible with ethanol-blended fuels

🔹 Advantages:

  • Long life span
  • No rusting
  • Lower maintenance

📌 Now widely used in new-generation retail outlets.

4️⃣ Compartmentalized Tanks

🔹 Description:

Single physical tank divided internally into 2–3 compartments.

🔹 Purpose:

  • Store MS & HSD in same tank structure
  • Store multiple grades (e.g., normal & premium MS)

🔹 Benefits:

  • Space-saving
  • Lower installation cost
  • Efficient underground space utilization

5️⃣ Underground Tanks with Automatic Tank Gauging (ATG)

🔹 Description:

USTs integrated with electronic monitoring systems.

🔹 Features:

  • Real-time stock monitoring
  • Temperature correction
  • Leak detection alerts
  • Density tracking

📌 Common in modern fuel stations and COCO outlets.

📊 Standard Underground Fuel Tank Capacities in India

(Retail Outlets – MS / HSD / Branded Fuels)

Fuel retail outlet tanks in India are installed as per layout approval under the Petroleum Rules, 2002 and safety licensing by the Petroleum and Explosives Safety Organisation (PESO).

🛢️ 1️⃣ Small Capacity Tanks

(Used in rural outlets / low sales volume stations)

  • 5 KL (5,000 Litres)
  • 10 KL (10,000 Litres)

📌 Mostly single-compartment tanks.

🛢️ 2️⃣ Medium Capacity Tanks

(Most common at standard retail outlets)

  • 12 KL
  • 15 KL
  • 18 KL
  • 20 KL

📌 Very common configuration: 15 KL (MS) + 15 KL (HSD)

🛢️ 3️⃣ Large Capacity Tanks

(Urban / Highway / High sales outlets)

  • 25 KL
  • 30 KL
  • 35 KL
  • 40 KL

📌 Often compartmentalized for MS Normal, MS Premium, HSD.

🛢️ 4️⃣ High Volume / COCO / Highway Mega Outlets

  • 45 KL
  • 50 KL
  • 60 KL

Used at:

  • Highway corridors
  • High turnover company-owned outlets
  • Bulk consumer points

🛢️ 5️⃣ Compartmentalized Tank Configurations

Single tank body divided internally into sections. Examples:

  • 30 KL (15 KL + 15 KL)
  • 45 KL (15 KL + 15 KL + 15 KL)
  • 40 KL (20 KL + 20 KL)
  • 30 KL (10 KL + 10 KL + 10 KL)

📌 Helps store multiple products within limited space.

🛢️ 6️⃣ Special Purpose Tanks

  • Ethanol-blended fuel compatible tanks
  • Bio-diesel storage tanks
  • Branded fuel separate tanks (e.g., premium MS)

Usually 10–20 KL depending on demand.

🧮 Why Diameter Changes with Capacity

Tank volume formula (horizontal cylinder):

Volume = π r² L

Where:

  • r = radius
  • L = length

Manufacturers balance diameter & length to:

  • Maintain structural strength
  • Fit underground pit dimensions
  • Maintain safe burial depth
  • Allow proper manhole spacing

📌 Practical Field Insight

Most commonly seen tanks in Indian retail outlets:

  • 15 KL → 2.2 m dia × 4 m length
  • 20 KL → 2.5 m dia × 4 m length
  • 30 KL → 2.75 m dia × 5 m length
  • 40 KL → 2.75–3 m dia × 6 m length

These are widely adopted by OMC-approved fabricators.

⚖️ Regulatory & Safety Compliance

USTs must comply with:

  • Petroleum Act, 1934
  • Petroleum Rules, 2002
  • PESO approval & licensing
  • Approved layout plan
  • Fire safety norms

🔎 Key Inspection Points for Officers

  • Tank number matches dip chart
  • Proper earthing & bonding
  • Manhole covers sealed
  • Water accumulation check
  • Calibration certificate available
  • Leak detection system functioning
  • Tank capacity matches PESO licence
  • Dip chart corresponds to actual tank dimensions
  • No unauthorized capacity modification
  • Proper coating (MS tanks)
  • FRP thickness certification (if composite tank)
  • Compartment marking clearly displayed
  • ATG calibration matches tank size
  • No overfilling beyond safe working capacity

📝 Summary

Underground fuel storage tanks at Indian retail outlets have evolved significantly over the years:

  • Types: From single-wall MS tanks to double-wall steel and FRP tanks with electronic monitoring
  • Capacities: Range from 5 KL (rural) to 60 KL (highway mega outlets), with 15 KL being the most common
  • Dimensions: Typical 15 KL tank measures 2.2m diameter × 4m length; larger tanks scale proportionally
  • Compliance: All tanks must meet PESO requirements under Petroleum Rules, 2002
  • Modern trend: Double-wall FRP tanks with ATG systems for enhanced environmental safety

Understanding these specifications is crucial for field officers, dealers, and enforcement teams conducting inspections at retail outlets.
