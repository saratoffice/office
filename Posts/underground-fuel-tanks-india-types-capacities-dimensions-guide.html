01 Tank Types

Types of Underground Fuel Tanks

Under Rule 116 of the Petroleum Rules 2002, underground storage tanks must be fabricated from iron, steel, fibre reinforced plastic, or other suitable material conforming to IS:803, IS:10987, API:650, or any equivalent standard accepted in writing by the Chief Controller of Explosives.

Mild Steel (MS) Single-Wall Tanks

IS:803 · IS:10987 · Horizontal Cylindrical · Pre-2005 Standard

MS Horizontal Tank
Lowering into RCC Pit
Epoxy Coated Surface
RCC Pit Installation

Traditional horizontal cylindrical steel tanks installed below ground in RCC pits with sand cushioning. The most common type found at older rural and semi-urban retail outlets built before 2005.

Fabricated from mild steel plates per IS:803 and coated with epoxy or coal tar for corrosion resistance. Cathodic protection may be provided in highly corrosive soil conditions.

IS:803 Fabrication Lower Capital Cost Widely Available Corrosion Susceptible Periodic Inspection Needed Soil Contamination Risk Cathodic Protection Possible
Predominantly found at rural outlets and older ROs. Earthing resistance ≤ 7 ohms mandatory (Rule 127, Petroleum Rules 2002).

Double-Wall Steel Tanks

Secondary Containment · Interstitial Leak Monitoring · 2005–Present

Double-Wall Steel Tank
Interstitial Monitoring
FRP-Jacketed Steel
Modern Double-Wall

Two-wall construction with an interstitial space between inner and outer steel walls. The inner wall stores fuel; the outer wall provides secondary containment. Leak detection sensors in the interstitial space alert operators before any ground contamination occurs.

A leak in the inner wall is detectable and contained by the outer shell, giving operators time to remediate without soil or groundwater impact — the key environmental advantage over single-wall MS tanks.

Early Leak Detection Zero Soil Contamination on Inner Leak Environmental Compliance Higher Initial Cost Secondary Containment Standard
Increasingly mandated at new DODO/COCO outlets post-2015. Interstitial sensors must be tested periodically as per OMC protocols.

Fiberglass Reinforced Plastic (FRP) Tanks

Corrosion-Free · E20 Compatible · New-Generation Standard

FRP Underground Tanks
FRP Tank Installation
FRP Storage Tank
FRP Tank Manufacturing

Non-metallic composite tanks made of fiberglass reinforced plastic. Inherently corrosion-free, lighter than equivalent steel tanks, and fully compatible with ethanol-blended fuels (E10, E20) — critical for India's ongoing ethanol blending programme.

Available in both single-wall and double-wall configurations. FRP double-wall tanks with integrated interstitial monitoring are the modern benchmark for new retail outlet construction. FRP thickness certification is mandatory per PESO guidelines.

100% Corrosion Resistant E10 / E20 Compatible 30+ Year Lifespan No Cathodic Protection Needed Lower Lifetime Maintenance FRP Thickness Certification Required
Now the preferred choice for new-generation outlets. Mandatory FRP thickness certification must be filed with PESO licence application.

Compartmentalized Tanks

2–3 Internal Partitions · MS + HSD in Single Structure · Space-Efficient

A single physical tank divided internally into 2 or 3 separate compartments, each independently dip-measured and calibrated. Stores multiple products — typically Motor Spirit (MS) and High Speed Diesel (HSD), or normal and premium MS grades — within one underground structure, saving significant excavation and pit construction costs.

Internal partitions slightly reduce effective storage length per compartment. Each compartment requires its own stamped dip chart, and compartment labels must be clearly marked at every manhole cover.

Space-Saving Design Lower Installation Cost Efficient Pit Utilisation Separate Dip Chart Per Compartment Compartment Labelling Mandatory Common: 30 KL (15+15)

Tanks with Automatic Tank Gauging (ATG)

Real-Time Electronic Monitoring · COCO / High-Volume Standard

Automatic Tank Gauging — What It Does

Any tank type (MS single-wall, double-wall steel, or FRP) can be integrated with an ATG console. The system uses a magnetostrictive or radar probe to provide continuous, electronic readings of fuel level, volume, temperature, and density — replacing manual dip measurement and dramatically reducing measurement error and stock discrepancies. ATG calibration must precisely match the actual tank dimensions on file; any mismatch is a compliance violation during inspection.

Real-Time Stock Level Temperature Correction (15°C ref.) Density / Density Variance Leak Detection Alerts Water Ingress Detection OMC Back-Office Integration Calibration Certificate Mandatory
02 Standard Capacities

Standard Underground Tank Capacities at Indian Retail Outlets

Tank installations are approved on a site-by-site basis under the Petroleum Rules 2002, through PESO licensing. Capacity planning reflects daily throughput projections, land area, and OMC norms. 03 Physical Dimensions

Typical Diameter & Length of Underground Tanks

USTs at Indian petrol pumps are horizontal cylindrical tanks. Per Rule 116(3) of the Petroleum Rules 2002, the height of a storage tank shall not exceed one and a half times its diameter. Fabricators balance diameter and length to maintain structural integrity per IS:803, fit excavated pit dimensions, and allow correct manhole and vent pipe placement.

04 Regulatory Compliance

Regulatory & Safety Compliance Framework

All underground storage tanks at Indian fuel retail outlets operate under a multi-layered statutory framework. PESO issues storage licences for 3 years — renewal must be applied for before expiry. No petroleum may be stored until the PESO licence has been granted and the mandatory water pressure test certificate (Rule 126, Petroleum Rules 2002) has been submitted.

Governing Legislation
  • Petroleum Act, 1934
  • Petroleum Rules, 2002 (Rules 116–130)
  • OISD-STD-244 — Petroleum Storage Depots
  • IS 5572:2009 — Hazardous Area Classification
  • Explosives Rules, 2008 (licence renewal)
Fabrication Standards
  • IS:803 — Design, Fabrication & Installation of Steel Tanks
  • IS:10987 — Underground Tanks for Petroleum
  • API:650 — Welded Steel Tanks for Oil Storage
  • OISD-STD-113 — Hazardous Area Classification
Mandatory Approvals & Certificates
  • PESO Licence — Form XIV (3-year validity)
  • PESO-approved site layout plan
  • Water pressure test certificate (Rule 126) before first use
  • ATG calibration certificate
  • Earthing continuity certificate
  • FRP thickness certification (if composite tank)
Safety Installation Requirements
  • Min. 1.5 m separation from boundary wall (Rule 116)
  • Tank vent minimum 4 m from any source of ignition
  • Earth resistance ≤ 7 ohms; fitting-to-earth ≤ 2 ohms (Rule 127)
  • Manhole covers sealed and inspected periodically
  • Cathodic protection for MS tanks in corrosive soil
  • Spark arrestors on all delivery vehicle exhaust
ℹ PESO Licensing — Key Timelines A PESO storage licence is valid for 3 years and must be renewed per Rule 165 of the Explosives Rules 2008, with applications submitted online via peso.gov.in. Any increase in tank capacity, change of tank type, or relocation requires a fresh PESO approval and an updated approved drawing before any petroleum is stored. The application process includes document scrutiny, site inspection, and endorsement by a PESO officer.
05 Inspection Checklist

Key Inspection Points for Field Officers

Tank number marked at manhole matches dip chart
Earthing resistance ≤ 7 ohms (Rule 127)

Leak detection / interstitial monitoring system functional

No unauthorized capacity modification

06 Evolution of USTs in India

How Underground Tank Technology Has Evolved

Pre-2005
Single-Wall MS Tanks
Coal tar or bitumen coating. Manual dip-stick measurement only. No interstitial leak monitoring. High risk of undetected seepage causing soil and groundwater contamination.
2005 – 2015
MS + Double-Wall Steel
Interstitial leak detection introduced at modern outlets. Epoxy coating standardized. Early ATG adoption at COCO sites. Environmental liability awareness increased post OISD-244 adoption.
2015 — Present
FRP & Double-Wall + ATG
Corrosion-free FRP preferred for new constructions. ATG mandatory at high-volume and COCO sites. Ethanol-blend (E20) material compatibility now required for all new installations under OMC norms.
✅ Current Best Practice New-generation retail outlets install double-wall FRP tanks with ATG systems and interstitial leak monitoring. This configuration meets environmental safety requirements, supports E20 blending, enables real-time stock reconciliation with OMC back-office systems, and significantly reduces long-term maintenance costs compared to single-wall MS tanks.

Key Takeaways

  • Types: Five main types are in service — from simple single-wall MS to sophisticated FRP double-wall with ATG monitoring
  • Capacities: Range from 5 KL (rural) to 60 KL (highway mega outlets); 15 KL is the most common across India
  • Dimensions: Most common 15 KL tank: 2.2 m diameter × 4 m length; tanks scale proportionally with capacity (V = πr²L)
  • Compliance: PESO licence mandatory under Petroleum Rules 2002; valid 3 years; water pressure test (Rule 126) required before commissioning
  • Earthing: Earth resistance must not exceed 7 ohms; fitting-to-earth resistance must not exceed 2 ohms (Rule 127)
  • Modern standard: Double-wall FRP + ATG + ethanol-blend compatibility is the benchmark for new retail outlet construction
