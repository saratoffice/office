🛢️ Petroleum Storage & Transport Rules (India)
Simplified Guide Based on Petroleum Act & Rules

Sarat Rout April 4, 2026 8 min read Industrial Safety
Petroleum rules classify inflammable liquids into Class A, B, C with varying safety requirements

Regulatory Framework

Under the Petroleum Act, 1934 and the Petroleum Rules, 2002 (as amended), storage, transport, and handling of petroleum are strictly regulated by the Chief Controller of Explosives, Petroleum and Explosives Safety Organisation (PESO). The rules classify petroleum into three hazard classes based on flash point, each with distinct container requirements, air space rules, licence exemptions, and safety protocols.

Applicability: Whole of India, with special exemptions for Defence Forces.

🔴 Petroleum Class A (Highly Inflammable)

Flash point below 23°C – e.g., Petrol, Naphtha

Container Requirements

Must be made of tinned, galvanized, or rust-proof steel/iron. Approved by Chief Controller of Explosives. Glass bottles allowed only up to 2.5 litres (lab use, approved).

Design & Safety

Airtight caps (screw plugs/caps), leak-proof and durable construction. Must always be in good condition.

Air Space Rule

Minimum 5% air space required in containers.

Labelling

Must clearly display: “Petrol / Motor Spirit – Highly Inflammable”

Licence Exemption (Small Quantity): No licence required if quantity ≤ 30 litres (not for sale). Storage conditions: Glass/stoneware ≤ 1 litre; Metal containers ≤ 25 litres.

Motor Vehicle Exemption

Fuel in tank → No licence needed. Additional storage allowed: Up to 100 litres (Class A) for own use. If >30 litres: must be stored in isolated location.

Exceptions (Class A)

Rules do not apply to containers owned by Defence Forces of India.

🟡 Petroleum Class B (e.g., Kerosene, Diesel Range)

Flash point 23°C to 65°C

Container Requirements

Must be made of steel or iron using approved containers.

Air Space Rule

Minimum 5% air space required.

Dispatch & Delivery Rules

Petroleum can only be delivered to: Licence holder or authorised agent | Port authority / railway administration | Person legally allowed without licence.

Special Rule (Without Licence): Can be delivered without licence if quantity ≤ 15,000 litres stored in airtight approved containers. Additional conditions: Receiver must dispose/use immediately. If quantity > 2,500 litres: must include dry chemical fire extinguisher.

Decantation Rule (Kerosene)

Allowed for licence holders. Must transfer into containers ≤ 2,500 litres immediately.

Licence Exemption (Storage & Transport)

No licence required if: Total quantity ≤ 2,500 litres & no container > 1,000 litres.

Exceptions (Class B)

Defence Forces of India; Kerosene supplied for licensed decantation purposes.

🟢 Petroleum Class C (Less Volatile Oils)

Flash point above 65°C – e.g., Furnace Oil, Lubricating Oil

Container Requirements

Must be made of approved steel or iron.

Air Space Rule

Minimum 3% air space required.

Licence Exemption: No licence required if total quantity ≤ 45,000 litres stored/transported as per approved container rules.

Exceptions (Class C)

Rules do not apply to containers owned by Defence Forces of India.

⚠️ General Restrictions on Delivery (All Classes)

Petroleum cannot be delivered to just anyone. Must match: Licence type & Permitted quantity. Sender must ensure safe handling and legal compliance.

Statutory References

These rules derive from the Petroleum Act, 1934 (Sections 4, 5, 6, 8, 11) and Petroleum Rules, 2002 (Chapters II, III, V, VI). The Chief Controller of Explosives has powers to enforce conditions, inspect premises, and grant exemptions in special circumstances. Non-compliance attracts penalties including imprisonment and fines under Section 10 of the Petroleum Act.

Always verify latest amendments through PESO (Petroleum and Explosives Safety Organisation) official notifications.
