Governance & Regulatory Risk Memos
KIRLOSBROS — Kirloskar Brothers Ltd
Risk Score: 7/10 — HIGH
Issue: SEBI, in October 2020, issued three separate orders against the Kirloskar family promoters (Atul, Rahul, and Sanjay Kirloskar factions) for insider trading and fraud against public shareholders. The investigation covered March 2010 – April 2011.

Material Facts: Six promoter entities sold 1.07 Cr shares (block deal) while in possession of unpublished price-sensitive information (UPSI) relating to Kirloskar Industries' decision to invest ₹275 Cr in KBL shares. Promoters submitted incorrect declarations claiming they held no UPSI. SEBI ordered disgorgement of ₹16.6 Cr (ill-gotten gains) plus ₹14.5 Cr penalty = ₹31.21 Cr total. Atul and Rahul Kirloskar were barred from markets for 6 months; Sanjay's entities for 3 months.

Current Status (2025–26): The case is old (2010 vintage) but the inter-family dispute between Sanjay vs Atul/Rahul factions remains an ongoing corporate governance risk. Market Smojo currently rates KIRLOSBROS as Sell with bearish technicals (Nov 2025 downgrade). The 6-month return is −22.41% with a fresh 52-week low in March 2026.

Investment Risk: Dual risk — historical governance concern and current bearish price momentum. Ongoing family tensions can resurface in board-level decisions affecting minority shareholders.
Primary: SEBI Order (Oct 2020) — https://www.sebi.gov.in | Business Standard (Oct 21, 2020) — https://www.business-standard.com | Moneylife (Oct 22, 2020)
⚠ VERDICT: AVOID for short-term. Governance overhang + bearish momentum + family dispute risk outweigh reasonable valuation. Revisit only on family resolution news or >30% further correction.
SHAKTIPUMP — Shakti Pumps (India) Ltd
Risk Score: 4/10 — MODERATE
Issue 1 (2010): SEBI fined 34 entities ₹1.5 Cr for synchronised and reversal trades in Shakti Pumps during Feb–Jul 2010, creating artificial volumes. Shakti Pumps' price surged 141% during the period vs Sensex +9.25%.

Issue 2 (2018): SEBI fined 8 entities ₹22 lakh for insider trading violations (March–October 2018). Designated employees Goutam, Sanjay and Mukesh Patidar traded without pre-clearances and executed contra trades within 6 months, gaining ₹9.19 lakh unlawfully. The company itself was penalised for not disgorging unlawful gains.

Issue 3 (Nov 2023): SEBI passed an order under SAST Regulations regarding Shakti Pumps' shareholding structure (specific details public on SEBI website, routine compliance matter).

Current Status: No active SEBI investigation as of 2025–26. Promoter family (Patidar) has clean chit on recent filings. Company has a Vigil Mechanism/Whistle Blower Policy in place.

Investment Risk: Historical issues are old (2010, 2018). Thin CFO/PAT (0.05) and negative net cash flow in FY25 warrant monitoring. Government order lumpiness creates quarterly earnings volatility.
Primary: SEBI SAST Order Nov 2023 — sebi.gov.in/enforcement/orders/nov-2023 | Zee News (Dec 3, 2022) | Business Standard (Sep 10, 2018)
✔ VERDICT: PROCEED WITH CAUTION. Past issues are historical and settled. Monitor CFO/PAT closely each quarter. Position size accordingly — this is a high-beta, high-reward play.
