⚖️ The Indian Judiciary is a single integrated system of courts that administers both central and state laws. It functions as an independent and impartial branch of government, ensuring justice and protecting the rights enshrined in the Constitution.

Supreme Court of India

Supreme Court of India
New Delhi · est. 28 Jan 1950

CJI + 33 judges (sanctioned strength 34). Retirement age 65. Guardian of the Constitution with power of judicial review.

  • Original / Appellate / Advisory jurisdiction
  • Court of Record — binding on all courts
  • Writ jurisdiction under Art. 32
Art. 124‑147Writ jurisdiction

High Courts & District Judiciary

High Courts

25 High Courts, oldest Calcutta HC (1862). Judges retire at 62. Supervisory powers over subordinate courts, writ jurisdiction Art. 226.

Art. 214‑231
District & Sessions

Principal court of original jurisdiction in each district. Head: District & Sessions Judge. Criminal (sessions) & civil divisions.

Art. 233‑235 CrPC, CPC

Subordinate courts & tribunals

Civil courts

Civil Judge (Sr./Jr.), Small Causes, Munsif

Sessions, JMFC, MM, CJM, Metropolitan Magistrates

Village courts

Nyaya Panchayats, Gram Nyayalayas, Lok Adalats

CAT, ITAT, DRT, NGT, consumer forums

types of jurisdiction

Original — first instance
Appellate — appeals from lower
Advisory — President’s reference
Writ — Art.32 & 226 (FR)
Revisory — call for records
Supervisory — HC over subordinate

evolution timeline

1773 Regulating Act — Supreme Court at Calcutta
1862 High Courts: Calcutta, Bombay, Madras
1935 Federal Court (Govt. of India Act)
1950 Supreme Court of India inaugurated
2008 Gram Nyayalayas Act

landmark supreme court judgments

Kesavananda Bharati (1973) – Basic Structure Doctrine
Maneka Gandhi (1978) – Art.21 expanded to dignified life
Minerva Mills (1980) – limited amendment power
Navtej Singh Johar (2018) – struck down Section 377

appointment & removal

Collegium system
CJI + 4 senior judges (SC). HC collegium: CJ + 2 senior judges.
Impeachment (Art.124(4))
Judges removed only for proved misbehaviour/incapacity; special majority.

key constitutional articles

Art.124-147 (SC) Art.214-231 (HC) Art.233-235 (districts) Art.32 (remedies)

🎯 conclusion

The Indian Judiciary, with the Supreme Court at its apex, ensures rule of law and protects fundamental rights through an integrated hierarchical system.
