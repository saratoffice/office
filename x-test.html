📋 Applicability Criteria

A factory license is mandatory if your manufacturing unit meets any of these conditions:

⚡ With Power

  • 10 or more workers
  • Manufacturing process using power
  • Any industry with electrical equipment

🔧 Without Power

  • 20 or more workers
  • Manual manufacturing process
  • Traditional/handicraft units

⚠️ Hazardous Industries

  • Chemical plants
  • Petroleum refineries
  • Explosive manufacturing
  • All sizes require license
💡 Note: Even if your unit has less than 10 workers but deals with hazardous processes, you MUST obtain a factory license under Chapter IV of the Factories Act.

💰 License Fee Structure

Fees vary by state and factory size. General guidelines under The Factories Act, 1948:

Factory Category Annual Fee Range Validity Renewal Fee
Small-scale (10-50 workers) ₹1,000 - ₹5,000 1-5 years Same as new
Medium-scale (51-250 workers) ₹5,001 - ₹15,000 1-5 years +15% penalty if delayed
Large-scale (251+ workers) ₹15,001 - ₹50,000 1 year +25% penalty if delayed
Hazardous industries ₹25,000 - ₹1,00,000 1 year Mandatory annual renewal
⚠️ Important: Fees vary by state. Check with your state's Directorate of Industrial Safety and Health (DISH) for exact amounts.

⏱️ Application Process Timeline

📌 Step 1: Document Collection (3-7 days)

Gather all required documents, prepare building plans, obtain NOCs

📌 Step 2: Form Submission (1 day)

Submit Form 1 along with documents to Chief Inspector of Factories

📌 Step 3: Scrutiny (7-10 days)

Department verifies documents, may request clarifications

📌 Step 4: Site Inspection (3-5 days)

Inspector visits factory to verify safety measures and working conditions

📌 Step 5: Fee Payment (1 day)

Pay prescribed fee after inspection approval

📌 Step 6: License Issuance (2-3 days)

License granted valid for specified period (1-5 years)

Total Processing Time: 15-30 working days typically

⚖️ Penalties for Non-Compliance

🚫 Operating without license

  • Imprisonment up to 2 years
  • Fine up to ₹1,00,000
  • Additional fine per day of continuing offense

📋 Safety violations

  • Fine up to ₹50,000
  • Imprisonment up to 6 months
  • Factory can be sealed

📊 Record keeping failures

  • Fine up to ₹25,000
  • Prosecution of manager/occupier

⚕️ Health/welfare violations

  • Fine up to ₹35,000
  • Renewal may be denied
⚠️ Important: In case of accident causing death/serious injury, penalties include imprisonment up to 7 years and fine up to ₹5,00,000 under Section 92 of Factories Act.

🔄 Renewal Process

When to renew:

  • Apply 60-90 days before expiry
  • Late renewal: File within 30 days of expiry with penalty
  • After 30 days: Fresh application required

Documents for renewal:

  • Previous year's license copy
  • Updated worker register
  • Annual return (Form 2) of previous year
  • Affidavit of no change in factory setup
  • Fee payment challan

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Who is the "occupier" under Factories Act?
The occupier is the person who has ultimate control over the affairs of the factory. In case of a company, any one of the directors can be designated as occupier. The occupier is personally responsible for compliance with the Act.
Can a factory license be transferred?
Yes, factory license can be transferred with prior permission from the Chief Inspector of Factories. Application for transfer must be made within 30 days of change of ownership/management.
What is the validity period of factory license?
Validity ranges from 1 to 5 years depending on state rules and category of factory. Hazardous industries typically get 1-year validity, while others may get up to 5 years subject to compliance history.
Is factory license required for a small workshop with 5 workers?
No, if it's a non-hazardous unit with less than 10 workers (with power) or 20 workers (without power), factory license is not required. However, other local municipal/industry licenses may be needed.
What are the working hour limits for adult workers?
No adult worker can work more than 9 hours a day and 48 hours a week. Rest interval of at least 30 minutes after 5 hours of work. Overtime paid at double the normal rate.
