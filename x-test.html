Fire Safety Precautions at a Retail Outlet (Petrol Pump)

Petroleum Act, 1934 Petroleum Rules, 2002 (Amd. 2024) OISD-STD-225 Gas Cylinders Rules, 2016 PESO Form XIV Licence
April 23, 2026 Sarat Rout 14 min read PESO — Apex Regulator  Fire Safety

A petroleum retail outlet handles Class A petroleum (motor spirit, flash point below 23 °C) and Class B petroleum (HSD, flash point 23–65 °C) — highly flammable hydrocarbons whose storage, handling, and dispensing are strictly regulated under the Petroleum Act, 1934, Petroleum Rules, 2002 (amended 2024), and OISD-STD-225. Every fire safety requirement at a petrol pump is statutory — not advisory — and carries legal consequences for non-compliance.

Licensing and Administrative Compliance

Every retail outlet must display its valid Form XIV licence prominently — with all licence conditions complied with at all times

Every petroleum retail outlet must operate under a valid Form XIV licence issued by PESO under the Petroleum Rules, 2002, authorising the storage of petroleum in underground tanks for fuelling motor conveyances. The licence must be displayed on premises at all times. Applications for renewal must reach the licensing authority on or before the date of expiry; if a timely application is made, the licence is deemed to continue until the renewal is granted or refused.

 Key Administrative Obligations — Form XIV
  • Staff training records: Records of periodic safety training, including mock fire drills, must be maintained and available for inspection.
  • Equipment certification: All fire extinguishers must bear a valid ISI/BIS mark, current pressure-test tag, and annual refilling record.
  • Hazard signage: "No Smoking," "Switch Off Engine," "No Mobile Phone Use," and emergency telephone numbers must be displayed in local language, Hindi, and English.
  • Rules extract: An extract of the applicable Petroleum Rules must be displayed at the outlet as required by the OISD-STD-225 daily checklist.
 Non-Compliance Consequence

Operating without a valid Form XIV licence, or in violation of any licence condition, is an offence under the Petroleum Act, 1934. PESO has the authority to suspend or cancel the licence. District authorities are also empowered to verify compliance and take necessary action.

Underground Storage Tanks — Design and Infrastructure

Underground Storage Tanks (USTs) store Class A and Class B petroleum at the retail outlet. Their design, installation, testing, and maintenance are the most critical fire and explosion prevention elements at any petrol pump.

 Tank Design & Leak Prevention

USTs must be leak-proof, constructed to approved specifications, and periodically tested for integrity. Fill pipes must have a standard thread size of 75 mm diameter. The diprod must be in good working condition and used regularly for manual stock verification.

 Vent Pipes & Flame Arrestors

Wire mesh / flame arrestors must be fitted to all vent pipe outlets. The wire gauge must not be clogged or missing — this is a critical fire prevention element specifically verified in the OISD-STD-225 daily checklist.

 Earthing & Static Electricity Control

All tanks, pipelines, dispensing units, and pump pedestals must be properly earthed to prevent static electricity build-up. Electrodes must be easily accessible for testing. Pump pedestals must be maintained with dry sand below.

 Safety Distances

Safety distances between retail outlet facilities and surrounding buildings, roads, and sensitive locations must comply with conditions specified in Form XV of the Petroleum Rules, 2002 and applicable licence conditions.

Electrical Safety and Hazardous Area Classification

The dispensing area is classified as a hazardous zone under OISD-STD-113, incorporated by reference in the Petroleum Rules, 2002. Class A petroleum vapour mixed with air in certain proportions forms a highly flammable mixture capable of igniting from any spark or heat source.

Flameproof Electrical Fittings in Hazardous Zone

All electrical fittings — wiring, switches, motors, junction boxes, light fittings — within the designated hazardous zone must be of the explosion-proof (flameproof) type, conforming to relevant IEC/IS standards. Tamper-proof junction boxes must be kept properly closed at all times.

Emergency Stop / Master Switch

A prominent "Master Switch" capable of cutting power to all dispensing units simultaneously must be installed at an accessible, clearly marked location — enabling rapid isolation of all dispensing activities in any emergency.

 OISD-STD-225 Daily Checklist — Electrical Items

Inspectors must verify: all dispensing unit connections and tank farm piping are leak-free with no sweating at joints; no loose wiring inside the pump pedestal; tamper-proof boxes are closed properly; and dry sand fills the gap below each pump pedestal. These are mandatory spot-check items during any enforcement inspection.

Control of Ignition Sources

Ignition source control is the first line of fire prevention at a retail outlet. Every possible source of spark, heat, or open flame must be rigorously excluded from the vicinity of dispensing and storage areas.

 No Smoking or Open Flames

Smoking, lighters, matchsticks, and any open flame are absolutely prohibited within the licensed area. Mandatory signage must be displayed and enforced without exception by all outlet staff.

 Engine Off During Fuelling

All vehicle engines must be switched off before fuelling begins. A running engine is both a heat source and a potential spark source that can ignite spilled petroleum vapour.

 Mobile Phone Restriction

Mobile phones must not be used near dispensing points — primarily to keep attendants focused on fuelling operations and eliminate distraction, the leading cause of fuel spills and overflow.

 No Unauthorised Hot Work

Welding, grinding, cutting, or any hot work requires prior authorisation, a formal permit-to-work, gas-free certification, and a dedicated fire watch throughout the entire operation.

Mandatory Fire-Fighting Equipment

OISD-STD-225 mandates 2 nos. of 10 kg DCP extinguishers per dispensing pump — maximum running distance to locate any extinguisher is 15 metres

Every licensed retail outlet must maintain specified fire-fighting equipment in type and quantity as prescribed by OISD-STD-225 and referenced under the Petroleum Rules, 2002. All extinguishers must conform to their respective Indian Standards and bear valid ISI/BIS marks.

🧯

DCP Extinguishers (Dry Chemical Powder)

Effective against Class B (flammable liquid) and Class C (electrical) fires — the primary hazard type at a petrol pump.

  • OISD-STD-225 2 nos. × 10 kg DCP per dispensing pump
  • Conforming to IS:2171 with valid ISI mark
  • Max top surface height: 1.5 m above ground level
  • Max running distance to locate: 15 metres
  • Rain protection mandatory; refilled annually or after use
🪣

Sand Buckets

Dry sand smothers small liquid fuel fires by cutting off oxygen and absorbing spilled fuel.

  • OISD-STD-225 8-litre capacity, round bottom, max 10 nos.
  • Sand must be fine, dry, and free from oil, water, or rubbish
  • Rain protection mandatory; last verification date on record
⚡

CO₂ Extinguishers (Carbon Dioxide)

Essential for electrical fires — CO₂ leaves no residue and is safe on live electrical equipment.

  • Mandatory near electrical panels, switchboards, DG rooms, sub-stations
  • OISD-STD-118: 1 no. 4.5 kg CO₂ per 25 m² of switch room
  • Conforming to IS:2878 with valid ISI mark
🫧

Foam Extinguishers

Foam blankets burning fuel, cutting off oxygen and cooling the fuel surface.

  • Required near tank lorry unloading points and UST areas
  • Foam compound per IS:4989; periodically tested per OISD-STD-142
  • Different foam concentrate types must never be mixed
 OISD-STD-225 Daily Checklist — Equipment Items

Inspectors must verify: fire extinguishers in position, charged, and within validity; sand buckets containing dry sand (not oil-contaminated or water-logged); and both extinguishers and sand buckets positioned near the tank lorry during unloading. Mandatory items in any enforcement inspection of a retail outlet.

Tanker Unloading — Safety Protocol

Tank lorry (TL) unloading is the highest-risk operation at a retail outlet — it involves the transfer of large volumes of Class A or Class B petroleum under pressure, with significant vapour release. The Petroleum Rules, 2002 and OISD-STD-225 prescribe a strict protocol for this operation, every step of which is verifiable during an inspection.

Bonding Cable Must Be Connected Before Decanting Begins

The tank lorry must be connected to a dedicated bonding (earthing) cable before decanting begins, to discharge static electricity accumulated during transit. The OISD-STD-225 checklist asks specifically: "Is bonding wire connected while decanting the tank lorry?" Failure to bond is a critical safety lapse that must be immediately flagged during any inspection.

Tank Lorry Engine Must Be Switched Off Throughout

The engine of the tank lorry must remain switched off for the entire duration of the decanting operation. A running tanker engine is both a heat source and a potential ignition source for petroleum vapour released during transfer.

Authorised Personnel Only at the Decanting Area

Only trained and authorised personnel — the tank lorry driver and the designated Delivery Station Manager (DSM) — should be present and active during unloading. Bystanders must be kept away from the decanting area at all times.

No Dispensing to Vehicles During Active Unloading

No fuel should be dispensed to vehicles during active tanker unloading, to prevent vapour pressure disturbances in the underground tank system that increase the risk of spills and overflow at the dispensing point.

Spillage Must Be Absorbed Immediately — Never Washed Away

Any spillage during unloading must be immediately absorbed using dry sand or approved absorbents. Spilled petroleum must never be washed into the drainage system — it must be collected and safely disposed of. Drainage must prevent accumulation of flammable vapour in low-lying areas.

 Pre-Unloading Verification — OISD Checklist

Before authorising tanker unloading verify: (a) bonding cable connected; (b) fire extinguishers and sand buckets positioned near the TL discharge point; (c) tanker engine off; (d) discharge points distinctively painted as per approved standard; (e) dispensing to vehicles suspended; and (f) lorry discharge points and dip pipes securely closed after completion.

Operational Safety Practices

Day-to-day operations must be carried out by trained personnel following established safe operating procedures. The OISD-STD-225 specifies both a daily checklist and a periodic checklist covering all safety elements of the outlet.

Housekeeping

Dustbins, garbage disposal, cotton/waste disposal, and regular cleaning of drainage channels must be maintained. Spilled fuel must be immediately mopped up. No flammable materials — LPG cylinders, cardboard cartons — should be stored in the dispensing area.

Dispensing Unit Cleanliness

All dispensing units must be kept externally and internally clean. The sand below each dispensing unit must be free of product spillage and any inflammable waste at all times.

Drainage Channel Maintenance

Parapets (drainage channels) must be clean, not leaking, and maintain a proper gradient to allow drainage of any spilled fuel away from dispensing and storage areas and towards a safe collection point.

Only Trained Staff on Duty

All operations — fuelling, unloading, stock measurement — must be performed by trained outlet staff familiar with the applicable safety rules, the daily checklist procedure, and emergency protocols.

CNG and Auto-LPG Dispensing — Additional Requirements

Retail outlets that also dispense Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) or Auto-LPG are subject to the additional requirements of the Gas Cylinders Rules, 2016, with CNG station licences issued in Form G under those Rules. PESO's March 2025 guidelines for CNG stations are clear on the following mandatory requirements:

All Passengers Must Disembark Before Filling Begins

Gas Cylinders Rules, 2016 and PESO guidelines are unambiguous: "There shall not be any passenger while refueling." Every passenger must disembark before CNG or Auto-LPG filling begins. Outlet attendants must enforce this rule for every vehicle without exception.

Engine Off and Vehicle Not Left Unattended

"The engine shall be in switch off mode while refueling" at CNG/Auto-LPG dispensing points. The vehicle must also not be left unattended while the filling operation is in progress.

Hydro-Test Validity Must Be Verified Before Dispensing

CNG cylinders fitted to vehicles must undergo periodic hydrostatic testing at recognised testing stations. Attendants must verify that the vehicle's CNG cylinder is within its valid hydro-test period before dispensing. An expired hydro-test date is grounds to refuse filling.

Never Exceed the Rated Maximum Working Pressure

CNG cylinders must never be filled beyond the rated maximum working pressure (MWP) stamped on the cylinder. Any leaking CNG cylinder valve must be moved to an open area away from ignition sources and the city gas distribution company must be immediately informed.

 CNG Hose Testing — PESO Guidelines (March 2025)

CNG dispensing hoses must be tested per the Gas Cylinders Rules, 2016 at prescribed intervals. Hose condition — cracks, bulges, or connector corrosion — must be checked regularly. A damaged CNG hose under operating pressure is an extreme fire and explosion hazard; it must be replaced immediately and the dispenser taken out of service.

Emergency Preparedness and Reporting

A written Emergency Action Plan (EAP) specific to the retail outlet must be prepared, approved, and kept updated. The plan must cover actions in the event of a fire, fuel spill, or gas leak; evacuation routes and assembly points; responsibility assignments; and the procedure for alerting external emergency services.

Emergency Contact Numbers Must Be Displayed Prominently

Emergency telephone numbers — local fire brigade, police, nearest hospital, PESO regional office — must be displayed at multiple locations within the outlet. The OISD-STD-225 checklist specifically asks: "Are safety messages like Tel. No., Police, Fire Brigade, Hospital and No Smoking, TL under decantation and Explosive Licence No. displayed?"

Mock Drills and Staff Training Records

Regular mock fire drills must be conducted and records maintained. All staff must be trained on how to operate each type of fire extinguisher, use sand buckets effectively, follow evacuation procedures, and safely deal with a fuel spill or CNG leak. PESO may inspect training records during regulatory audits.

Mandatory Reporting of Any Accident, Fire, or Leakage

Any accident, fire, explosion, or leakage at the retail outlet must be immediately reported to PESO and to local authorities (police, fire brigade). Failure to report is an independent violation of the Petroleum Rules, 2002. The outlet must cooperate fully with any subsequent investigation or enquiry.

 2025 Update — EV Charging at Retail Outlets

PESO has issued guidelines in 2025 for the installation of Electric Vehicle (EV) charging and battery swapping facilities at petroleum retail outlets. Outlets adding EV facilities must obtain prior approval from PESO and comply with specific safety separation distances and electrical safety requirements before commissioning any EV charging infrastructure on licensed premises.

🔥 Conclusion — Fire Safety is Statutory, Not Optional

  • The OISD-STD-225 daily checklist is the most practical enforcement tool — it systematically covers every critical safety parameter from tank integrity to fire extinguisher charging dates, and every item in it is legally verifiable during a PESO or ACSO inspection.
  • Fire-fighting equipment minimums are non-negotiable: 2 nos. × 10 kg DCP per dispensing pump; 8-litre sand buckets (max 10 nos.); CO₂ extinguishers at all electrical panels; foam extinguishers near unloading areas.
  • Tanker unloading is the highest-risk operation — bonding cable connection, engine-off, and suspension of vehicle dispensing are all mandatory and independently verifiable at inspection.
  • CNG stations face the additional obligations of the Gas Cylinders Rules, 2016 — passenger disembarkation, engine-off, hydro-test verification, and MWP compliance are enforceable at every fill.
  • Non-compliance with any mandatory requirement must be documented and referred to the appropriate authority for action under the Petroleum Act, 1934 — PESO has the power to suspend or cancel the Form XIV licence.
